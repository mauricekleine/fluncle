# Maurice Voice

Source basis: `/tmp/maurice-operating-system/brand/VOICE.md`, `/tmp/maurice-operating-system/brand/PLATFORM-VOICES.md`, `/tmp/maurice-operating-system/docs/VOICE.md`, and Maurice's LinkedIn post archive.

## Core Voice

Casual, direct, internet-native. Write like a builder talking to other builders.

Prioritize:

- Clarity over polish.
- Progress over perfection.
- Proof over hype.
- Specifics over positioning.
- Work-in-progress honesty over polished announcements.

## Pillars

### Builder-first

Write from inside the process.

Use:

- "we shipped"
- "I tried"
- "this broke"
- "we changed direction"
- "the numbers made it obvious"

Avoid:

- "we are excited to announce"
- "we are proud to introduce"
- "our innovative solution"
- "this marks a major milestone"

### Proof over hype

Let facts carry the post.

Good proof:

- A concrete number.
- A specific constraint.
- A named tool.
- A before/after.
- A weird bug or learning.
- Three concrete examples of pain.

Bad proof:

- "people love it"
- "the future is here"
- "enterprise-ready"
- vague "traction"

### Understated confidence

State the point plainly. Do not brag. Do not perform humility.

Better:

- "the math did not work"
- "we pivoted"
- "this made me pause"
- "I was wrong about this"

Worse:

- "I cannot believe we achieved this"
- "we are humbled and thrilled"
- "game-changing"

### Curious tinkerer

Show experiments, broken assumptions, and fast learning. The voice should feel like someone who tests ideas in public and keeps moving.

Use verbs like:

- ship
- build
- fix
- break
- test
- launch
- tweak
- experiment
- hack
- iterate
- pivot

Avoid:

- leverage
- synergy
- robust
- scalable
- next-generation
- transformative
- revolutionary
- seamless
- all-in-one

## Style Rules

Use:

- Short lines.
- One idea per paragraph.
- Sentence fragments when they improve rhythm.
- Lists for concrete examples.
- First person for founder posts.
- Contractions in social posts.
- Occasional lowercase in X posts.

Avoid:

- Long paragraphs.
- Em dashes.
- Hashtag stacks.
- Corporate closers.
- Overexplaining the obvious after numbers already make the point.
- The structure "X isn't just Y. It's Z."

## Signature Patterns

### Build-in-public hook

```
We built the wrong thing.

Not because the tech was bad.
Because the product assumption was.
```

### Concrete pain list

```
Every demo ended in the same three questions:

- where do I paste the API key?
- why does OAuth need another redirect URL?
- do I really need to SSH into this thing?
```

### Builder lesson

```
The useful lesson was not "agents are hard."

It was more specific:

agents stop being simple once they need files, tools, secrets, and memory.
```

## North Star Test

Before publishing, ask:

- Would Maurice say this out loud?
- Does it sound like someone building, not someone branding?
- Is the point concrete enough to disagree with?
- Is there proof, context, or a real example?
- Would this feel native on the platform?
