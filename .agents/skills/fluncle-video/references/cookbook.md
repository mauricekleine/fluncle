# Technique cookbook

Technique, not style. This teaches how to draw the things Fluncle videos are made of; the _what_ is yours, chosen per track. There is no committed track archive ‚Äî use these patterns as a vocabulary, and consult R2 output bundles only when the operator provides a specific historical composition. The exact `GLSL.*` signatures and the `ShaderLayer` injected header live in `packages/video/README.md`; this file assumes you have read that section.

The unit of drawing is one `ShaderLayer` rendering a GLSL fragment shader. Inside a shader you get the injected uniforms (`u_time`, `u_res`, `u_progress`, `u_energy`, `u_bass`, `u_mid`, `u_treble`, `u_beatPulse`, `u_seed`, `u_palette[4]`, plus the `*Fast` variants, the `u_audio*` composites, the FINE bands `u_sub`/`u_kickHit`/`u_snareHit`/`u_air`, and `u_downbeatPulse` ‚Äî full list in README) and the dither helpers (`ditherValue`, `dither8`). **GOTCHA: declare ONLY your own CUSTOM uniforms in the shader body ‚Äî NEVER re-declare these injected ones.** Re-declaring `uniform float u_time;` (etc.) is a GLSL `redefinition` compile error, and a failed compile renders the ShaderLayer ERROR OVERLAY instead of your scene (tell: a ~1MB output file ‚Äî far too small for a real grained render). Always frame-verify your final MP4; a confident "it works" is worthless if you never looked. You spread the `GLSL.*` snippet strings ahead of `void main()` and respect their dependencies (`fbm` needs `valueNoise`; `valueNoise`/`simplexNoise`/`filmGrain` need `hash`). **At the GPU level, bake grain and the palette ramp into the shader** (`GLSL.filmGrain`, `GLSL.paletteRamp`). The core ships NO CSS `Grain` overlay ‚Äî the recovered-footage grain is yours, in-shader, on every frame, and varied per video (the Light-Years Rule).

## fbm haze / smoke fields

A two-layer fbm ‚Äî a warp field flowing a second field ‚Äî gives haze its volume so it drifts and folds like stage haze instead of scrolling. Keep time slow. Map the result low through `paletteRamp` so the background stays dark and never reaches cream: it must stay a deep warm-dark cloud so the vehicle is always the brightest thing. Drive the bloom width with `u_energy` / `u_drop`.

For a GEL-SPLIT field, let two committed light temperatures share one frame: warm heat field vs a minor artwork counter-accent under the Retint Rule, with a soft breathing boundary. The drop can flood the field warm so the cool burns off. The shader recipe is warp fbm + field fbm + seam math + low ramp cap + screened-in cool wisp, then `vignette` ‚Üí `filmGrain` ‚Üí `dither8`.

## ridge / contour fields (the lines vehicle)

Line displacement as terrain or signal ‚Äî the Unknown Pleasures motif, data-as-landscape. Stack horizontal lines and displace each by a field; drive the displacement amplitude from the energy/bass/onset curves so the terrain reacts. Lines go in the scene's ink (Starlight Cream is the safe default); the climax is the lines' OWN material intensifying at the music's strongest structural moment ‚Äî brightening, thickening, the displacement deepening ‚Äî in the scene's palette (doctrine 1). Eclipse Gold is welcome only when the crest's own material genuinely runs hot, as one accent among the scene's colours; a cool line-field climaxes cool. Never bolt a separate gold bar or glow on top.

For a waveform-ridge terrain, let the stacked contour field travel monotonically and ride amplitude from broad energy plus tighter bass/onset curves. The crest intensifies at the music's strongest structural moment, not automatically in the center of the clip.

## dither / halftone resolution fronts (the glitch vehicle)

Order resolving out of corruption: the frame departs as scattered 1-bit dither noise, a corruption front travels across it resolving cell by cell through the drop, and arrives snapped into place ‚Äî a clean halftone form with a single burning rim. The whole texture can be ONE shader: halftone dither, the resolving front (a smoothstep edge swept by `u_progress`/`u_drop`), the SDF body, grain, and the palette ramp all baked in. The climax is the resolution front completing ‚Äî the vehicle's own material intensifying, in the scene's palette; gold rim only when that material genuinely runs hot, never a separate glow.

Keep this as one shader when possible: halftone dither + resolving front + SDF body + rim, all in one `ShaderLayer`. The resolving front is the vehicle; do not add a second light source to explain it.

## SDF celestial bodies (the orb vehicle)

A rendered-grade body, not a flat disc: an SDF circle whose body is an fbm-textured material, a hot fresnel-style limb (a thin bright burning edge), a soft outer glow with physical falloff, and emulsion grain baked INTO the surface so the grain reads as the material. Keep the body capped below the cream stop so it reads as a burning grainy body, not a washed-out white ball ‚Äî only the limb reaches the brightest stop, and the hot accent lives on the rim (~10% of the frame). For an eclipse, keep it the `limb` look (body in shadow along a terminator, one edge burning) rather than a full lit ball. The orb's heat colour is the scene's ‚Äî Eclipse Gold when the body genuinely runs hot, the scene's own hue (a deep-blue limb, a steel surge, a teal bloom) when it does not; the orb's climax is the limb intensifying, never a separate sun glued behind it.

**The quad law lives here (doctrine 6).** The exp glow falloff never reaches 0, so at the square layer's edge it prints a visible rectangle. Drive the glow to EXACT zero well inside the quad with a radial `edgeFade` (e.g. `1.0 - smoothstep(0.80, 0.96, r)`), and/or mask the layer to a circle in CSS ‚Äî a glow falloff that never reaches zero prints a visible rectangle at the quad edge, so do not skip this. **This radial fade is ONLY for a localized layer like the orb** (one that does not cover the frame). A full-bleed background field does the opposite: it fills to the corners, so a radial vignette there just crops the frame into a porthole (doctrine 6).

Key details: limb, shadowed body, core bloom, glow falloff, and an `edgeFade` or CSS radial mask that forces alpha to zero before the quad edge. A useful variant is a small ember that grows into the full limb on a late drop.

## beat mapping patterns

Audio reactivity comes ONLY from the curve hooks / shader audio uniforms ‚Äî never decode audio at render time, never wall clock.

- **Pulse decay on the beat grid:** `useBeat(beatGrid, { decay })` ‚Üí `pulse` snaps to 1 on each beat and decays before the next. `u_beatPulse` in shaders. The workhorse for rim flares and scale kicks. Higher `decay` is snappier (a neuro stomper); lower breathes (a roller).
- **Energy gates for drops:** the analyzer ships `audio.dropMs` (the detected drop) and `audio.dropCandidates`, so prefer the `reactivity.drop` envelope (which DEFAULTS its peak to `audio.dropMs`) over hand-deriving a window from the energy curve. If you do need a raw scalar, build one (an `interpolate` ramp across `[dropIn, dropOut]`, optionally floored so the intro is never stone-cold) around `audio.dropMs`. Gate ignition, field warmth, and exposure on it so the material peak and the visual peak land together.
- **Onset flashes:** `useOnset(onsets, windowMs)` ‚Üí a 0..1 spike per transient. Use for grain kicks, a dither threshold jolt, a chromatic-aberration shudder, a star twinkle ‚Äî supporting accents, never the vehicle.
- **Slow gestures:** `useEnergy` (smoothed, broad) drives starfield drift speed, glow breadth, global float; `useBass` (tighter) drives rim swell and breathing.
- **Frequency bands ‚Äî map instruments to elements (the music-driven win):** the three broad bands are analysed and fed as uniforms (`u_bass` <150Hz kick/sub, `u_mid` 150Hz-2kHz lead/vocal/snare, `u_treble` >2kHz hats/cymbals/air) and React hooks (`useBass` / `useMid` / `useTreble`, plus `*Fast` near-raw variants on the bus: `bassFast`/`midFast`/`trebleFast`), and beside them the FINE bands (`u_sub`/`useSub` <60Hz mass, `u_kickHit`/`useKick` the 60-150Hz kick STRIKE, `u_snareHit`/`useSnare` the 2-5kHz snare CRACK, `u_air`/`useAir` >5kHz sparkle ‚Äî the last three transient-emphasized, shared-normalized so cross-band loudness survives) plus `u_downbeatPulse`/`useDownbeat` on the bar. Drive DIFFERENT elements off DIFFERENT bands ‚Äî the kick strike (`u_kickHit`) fattens the core, the snare crack (`u_snareHit`) snaps a second element, the lead bends the flow, the air sparkles the grain. Reacting to one `energy` scalar reads as "moves with the music"; splitting the bands reads as "plays the music." Pick the band that matches what each element should feel. (For the track's OVERALL character ‚Äî bassy vs bright vs busy ‚Äî read `track.features` from props, the enrichment spectral summary: it tells you which band leads and how heavy/sparkly the whole vehicle should be before you even look at the per-frame curves.)
- **Kick emphasis (power-scaling):** `pow(u_bass, 4.0)` in-shader makes only strong kicks approach 1 (a real hit has to build before it reads) ‚Äî the standard audio-reactive trick for a punchy, non-mushy response. Temporal smoothing lives in the hooks (`smoothingFrames`, attack/decay) and in the pre-smoothed `swell`/`drop` busses: a fragment shader has no previous frame to lerp against (Remotion renders frames independently), so accumulate-over-time smoothing happens in React, not the shader.
- **Deriving drop timestamps:** the analyzer already ships `audio.dropMs` (the primary detected drop) and `audio.dropCandidates` (all scored), plus `audio.downbeats` for bar timing ‚Äî reach for those first. Scan `audio.energyCurve` for the peak(s) yourself only when you want a window the detector didn't surface; either way those are clip-progress positions you reuse for the drop gate and the type timing.

Build explicit beat maps with named scalars such as `drop`, `dropRise`, and `cold`, plus `DROP_IN`/`DROP_OUT` windows derived from the analyzed curve. For breakdown-led cuts, keep the vehicle visible in the quiet section and reserve the largest structural change for the late drop.

- **Evolve with the music (doctrine 10) ‚Äî read the arc from the live envelopes.** The clip's structural change rides the track's own arc, read live: drive density, threshold, width, ignition, exposure, and palette lean from `u_energy` / `u_audioSwell` / `u_audioDrop` directly, so the build, crest, and breakdown are the music on every track (warp-weave and plasma-current-web read their whole arc this way). Place the crest with the `reactivity.drop` envelope config (`{ peakTimeMs, riseMs, holdMs, fallMs }`) ‚Äî that derives the smoothed `u_audioDrop` from the energy curve, the sanctioned home for the crest timestamp. Reserve `interpolate(sec, [a, b], ‚Ä¶, { easing: Easing.inOut(Easing.cubic), ‚Ä¶ })` for the journey BASELINE (drift, a slow open, type seams) ‚Äî the steady clock the audio bends. Easing keeps each change glassy; the structure itself follows the envelopes, not a `sec` index pinned to one drop time.
- **The reactive scene-change (structural arc) ‚Äî the prized pattern on beat tracks.** Make the vehicle's whole CHARACTER transition across the song's sections ‚Äî its density, palette lean, internal-motion regime, brightness, and texture all shifting from the calm BUILD, through the DROP, into the vibrant MAIN part ‚Äî read from `u_energy` and the drop envelope, so the clip feels like it MOVES THROUGH the song: calm-before, vibrant-after. Drive the whole regime change off the live envelopes (a sparse, dim, slow-warping field in the breakdown that floods denser, brighter, and faster-deforming once the drop hits ‚Äî all in place, translation still the constant clock per doctrine 7), not a per-section `sec` index. This is the operator's most-wanted aliveness signature and it ONLY appears on tracks with a real beat (a track with no drop has no sections to cross). Distinguish it from "the copy-paste climax" failure mode: that one is about WHERE the single bright moment lands; the scene-change is about the whole composition's character TRANSITIONING across the build/drop/main sections ‚Äî a different, larger gesture than one well-placed pop. Exemplars (attributed by log ID): `018.5.7Y.judge` (a clear build‚Üídrop‚Üímain scene-change, layered cloud-gradient voronoi ‚Äî "BEST voronoi yet, almost electric") and `019.3.9J.judge` ("calm waters before the drop, alive and vibrant once it hits").

## motion, depth, and the moving climax

Three recurring failure modes, with the technique to avoid each (full framing in SKILL.md):

- **Split the two motion channels: GLOBAL translation is an audio-free clock, ALL reactivity is INTERNAL (doctrine 7).** A full-screen vehicle carries two channels. GLOBAL TRANSLATION (the whole vehicle drifting past) is a CONTINUOUS, one-way, audio-free constant-speed clock from `u_time` / `arc` / a steady eased rise ‚Äî PREFER audio off it entirely. INTERNAL DEFORMATION (the material morphing, lighting, extending, inhaling/exhaling, flowing in place) is where ALL the reactivity belongs, and it may be as sharp as the kick. RIGHT: `vec2 drift = driftDir * (u_time * 0.085);` (audio-free global clock) carrying a field whose `warpAmp` / `thr` / `width` ride the bands in place; or, if audio MUST bend translation, `float flow = radius*3.0 - u_time*0.85 - u_rise*1.4 - u_swell*0.6;` ‚Äî the constant `u_time` term stays DOMINANT and the audio coefficient is SMALL and SMOOTHED (energy envelope, bar swell, the drop, or a beat run through attack/decay), so velocity never reverses. WRONG: `float drift = u_audioSwell * 0.10;` (no constant base ‚Üí drift velocity = `0.10¬∑d(swell)/dt` ‚Üí reverses whenever swell dips ‚Üí DJ scratch), `drift = sec*0.85 + swell*1.4 + drop*0.5` (the audio coefficient rivals the constant clock ‚Üí the uncapped swell surges the whole vehicle), `... - u_hit*0.6` on a travel term, or `u_time * (1.0 + u_beatPulse)` on a speed term. Keep the beat OUT of motion ENTIRELY: `swell` is bass/energy-led and its per-beat weight (`swellBeatWeight`) defaults to **0**, because even a _smoothed_ per-beat still ticks the picture forward at high BPM ‚Äî proven on 173 BPM D&B, where zeroing it looked markedly smoother than a faint 0.14. Let the BASS carry the rhythmic feel of any motion you do allow (it's smoothed, so it surges without snapping); the beat itself belongs in MATERIAL, never movement. Don't raise `swellBeatWeight`, and don't fold `u_beatPulse`/`u_hit` into a travel/speed term to "make it dance." MATERIAL takes the immediate punch freely: `brightness *= 1.0 + u_hit*0.5`, `thickness *= 1.0 + u_bass*0.4`, `curvature += u_swell*k`, `radius *= 1.0 + u_hit*0.3`. So a kick makes a filament brighter / fatter at once while the whole field streams forward at a constant rate; it never jumps the field a step per kick. The exemplar is `cold-murmuration` (round-2 018.5.7Y): no audio on any translation term ‚Äî global motion is a pure constant clock, audio drives only the internal field. Keep streak frequency low (‚â≤ a dozen) or fbm-soften it so it doesn't strobe as it moves.
- **The climax is the vehicle igniting, not a sun glued on (doctrine 1).** Don't add a separate radial gold/colour bloom or halo behind or over the vehicle ‚Äî drive the vehicle's OWN material to its hottest stop at the climax, in the scene's palette: lerp the filament/blade/node/crest colour toward the scene's peak-heat hue where it's hottest (gate on the drop envelope). When that hue is genuinely gold, lerp toward `u_palette[2]`; when the scene is cool, the climax brightens in ITS OWN colour and reaching for yellow is the error. If deleting the vehicle would delete the bright moment, you've got it right.
- **Do NOT slow the grain to pass the gate ‚Äî that is the wrong knob.** The beat-pull gate is grain-HARDENED (it temporally pre-smooths, scoring MOTION, not grain), so slowing the grain clock does NOT lower the score ‚Äî it only DEGRADES the look. Recovered-footage grain BOILS (lively, fresh per-frame ‚Äî that liveliness IS the Light-Years grade); a slowed/crawling grain reads as smeared and dead, off-brand, and the operator catches it immediately. So leave `filmGrain` at full liveliness (`filmGrain(col, uv, u_time, amt)`); never reach for `u_time * 0.3` to game the gate. If the gate fails, the cause is the MOTION ‚Äî a per-beat signal on travel/position, or an intrinsically orbiting/breathing vehicle (see the curl-orbit note in organic fields) ‚Äî and the fix is always the motion wiring or the vehicle, never the grain. (This fences the grain BOIL/clock, which stays LIVELY ‚Äî each family its own rate, never crawled to game the gate; that is the gate-relevant knob. The grain AMOUNT (`intensity`) is the OPPOSITE: a FREE, composition-led knob ‚Äî bias it LOW for a clean, electric finding, since an over-applied grain flattens the pop. Vary amount and form per video; the boil stays lively.)
- **A still hero can't mask the grain's reversal floor ‚Äî give it bright DIRECTED motion.** The gate averages a per-window reversal RATIO (`1 ‚àí net/path`) over the WHOLE frame, and grain leaves a ~0.25 reversal FLOOR the temporal fence only half-removes (random per-frame noise barely travels, so net‚âàpath ‚Üí a high ratio). Bright content with DIRECTED motion (a one-way drift/flow ‚Üí high net) makes low-reversal windows that dominate the frame's mean abs diff and DILUTE that floor; bright content that is STATIC contributes ‚âà0 to the motion metric (no frame-to-frame change) and masks NOTHING ‚Äî the score then sits near the grain floor (~0.2) even when the picture visibly does NOT lurch. So a calm, smooth clip can still FAIL the gate if its hero doesn't move. The fix is not less grain (above) and not a per-beat pulse (that IS beat-pull) ‚Äî it's a slow ONE-WAY drift/flow on the dominant element (the smear / streaming families gate low for exactly this reason; a dead-centre static glow gates high). Proven on a gate-transit cut: a static bright core scored 0.20 (the grain floor showing through), and wrapping it in a one-way drifting luminous veil ‚Äî bright directed motion, no orbit, no beat ‚Äî dropped it to 0.14 with no change to the felt motion. This measurement is why the three-clocks law (doctrine 7) makes constant drift MANDATORY on an indifferent subject: presence scenes are exactly the ones tempted to lock the camera, and a locked-off hero fails here by construction.
- **The eye is the gate; the number is a proxy.** The gate scores _reversal_, so it reads HIGH on a smooth continuous spin or a periodic pattern sweeping past (each pixel rises and falls as features cross it ‚Äî a clean slow spin can hit ~0.16) and can read LOW on a busy, fine, fast-cycling field that visibly strobes. A green number is not automatically a pass and a high one is not automatically a fail. Rule on the rendered clip: scrub 4‚Äì5 adjacent frames around a few beats and confirm the picture GLIDES evenly ‚Äî broaden and slow a busy/fast field until it flows. The number is a cheap first filter; your eyes decide.
- **The gate punishes reversal, not evolution ‚Äî breathe by churning FORWARD.** A vehicle whose forms only rigidly translate (a frozen texture sliding one way) gates low but reads DEAD: it glides without breathing, the look the operator rejects. The cure is not to freeze the field ‚Äî it is to evolve it MONOTONICALLY. Advance the density/noise phase forward over time (a third "time" dimension fed into the `fbm` / `domainWarp` / `curl` inputs, distinct from the directed drift) so the rivers and forms roil and re-form IN PLACE as they stream. Forward-only evolution never returns ‚Üí no reversal ‚Üí the gate stays satisfied, and the picture is alive. Keep it SLOW and BROAD (fast/fine in-place churn flickers and bumps the gate; a light directional smear glues it). This is the breathing counterpart to the curl-orbit trap below: curl ORBITS (returns ‚Üí reversal); a monotonic phase advance FLOWS (never returns ‚Üí gate-safe).
- **The gate-safe binding idiom (in-place structural deformation ‚Äî the aliveness workhorse).** Bind the fast bands to the AMPLITUDE / THRESHOLD / WIDTH of an in-place deformation while the coordinate advance stays a constant-speed clock. The field churns harder on the kick but streams forward at a fixed rate: the structure deforms in place (no net translation to reverse) and the drift never returns, so this is gate-immune AND it may be as sharp as the kick. Three proven forms, attributed by primitive (steal the technique, not a scene):
  - **Warp AMPLITUDE on a band (any warped / marbled field).** `vec2 p = P + travelDir * (u_time * 0.045); float warpAmp = 0.12 + 0.16 * u_mid; float wd = (domainWarp(p * 1.8 + u_time * 0.060, 4) - 0.5) * warpAmp;` ‚Äî the lead bows the fabric HARDER in place while the coords advance on the clock. As sharp as you like: it never translates, so it never reverses.
  - **Ridge-survival THRESHOLD + filament WIDTH on the bands (ridged-filament networks).** `float open = clamp(u_audioSwell * 0.8 + u_energy * 0.45, 0.0, 1.0); float thr = mix(0.60, 0.49, open); float w = 0.15 + u_bass * 0.10 + u_audioDrop * 0.06; float fil = smoothstep(thr, thr + w, ridges);` ‚Äî the build lowers the survival gate so more tendrils survive; the kick fattens them ‚Äî both in place.
  - **Cell-wall WIDTH + voronoi wall sharpness on the swell (voronoi / cell fields).** `float w = 0.030 * (1.0 + u_audioSwell * 0.90 + u_audioDrop * 0.55); float crack = 1.0 - smoothstep(w * 0.40, w, edge);` over a voronoi field creeping on `u_time * k` ‚Äî the cracks widen on the swell, the wall edge stays sharp on the kick.
  - **The rule in one line:** coords advance on constant-speed time; audio drives the amplitude / threshold / width, never the coordinate translation. The `*Fast` bands (`u_bassFast` / `u_midFast` / `u_trebleFast`) belong here ‚Äî fast in, in place.
- **Depth, not diagram.** Volume comes from two-layer fbm, soft radial falloff, vignette, and layered haze ‚Äî not thin hard parallel primitives on a sparse field. If your field could pass for a UI element, widen it, soften the edges, and layer haze behind it until it reads as cosmos.
- **Move the climax.** Don't gate the climax to dead-centre on every clip. Scan `audio.energyCurve` for where the music ACTUALLY peaks and ignite there ‚Äî late, early, a slow swell held to the close, an off-centre crest, a front sweeping across. The centred mid-clip glow is the template to escape, not the target.

## organic fields: curl flow, cells, domain warp

The fluid/organic/alive north star now has helpers ‚Äî reach for these before hand-rolling:

- **`curlNoise(p)`** ‚Äî a divergence-free flow field. Advect a coordinate by it (`p += curlNoise(p * f) * amt`) for swirling, smoke-like, never-diverging motion: flocks, embers, ink, dust. Scale `p` for eddy size; evolve it on `u_time` and bend the strength with a smoothed band (Motion law).
  - **Orbit caveat (the elastic-breathing trap).** Curl is locally ROTATIONAL, so an advected point ORBITS and returns near where it started ‚Äî that is reversal (the "elastic, jumping in and out" look the beat-pull gate flags) EVEN WITH NO AUDIO. A flock that converges/disperses, or a plume that churns in place, breathes for the same reason. For motion that reads as FLOWING rather than pulsing, a DOMINANT directed/translational component must override the orbit ‚Äî a strong monotonic drift/updraft that carries the field forward so the net path streams instead of returning (e.g. raise the linear advance, lower the curl amplitude). If you need clean motion and the curl keeps breathing, that vehicle may be the wrong primitive ‚Äî a directed front, a translational sweep, or a field that evolves without returning will get clean far more easily than a curl-advected flock. (A surgical patch can damp this but rarely clears it; pick a non-orbiting primitive up front.)
- **`voronoi(p)`** ‚Äî cellular / worley crackle. Cell WALLS = `smoothstep(0.06, 0.0, vor.y - vor.x)` (cracked glaze, dry lakebed, cell membranes); cell FILL = `vor.z` (per-cell hash for varied tone). Breathe the cells on the bass; widen the walls on the drop. **This is the `cellular` structural family (doctrine 3), and as of 2026-07-04 it is SATURATED ‚Äî the operator's least-favourite and the basin the fleet keeps sliding back into.** It is a strong technique, not banned: reach for it only once `judge:diversity` shows the recent window has cleared of cellular. When you want "dense and alive" by default, reach instead for a family the window lacks ‚Äî `flow` (fbm / domain-warp), `caustic`, `filament` (ridged crests, below), `radial`, `lattice`, or `metaball`. Pick the family first, then name the world.
- **`domainWarp(p, octaves)`** ‚Äî fbm-of-fbm. Marbled, flowing fields that never read as gridded; the antidote to clean noise. Use as a base field or warp another field's coordinates through it.
- **ridged turbulence ‚Üí glowing FILAMENTS** (liquid-fire / plasma / veins / lightning / molten tendrils) ‚Äî the robust go-to when you want a dense, alive field. Warp an fbm field `n` (`domainWarp`, or `fbm` of curl-advected coords), RIDGE it ‚Äî `float ridges = pow(1.0 - abs(2.0 * n - 1.0), 2.7);` (k ‚âà 2.5‚Äì3) ‚Äî then THRESHOLD so only strong ridges survive as thin tendrils on a near-black field: `float fil = smoothstep(thr, thr + w, ridges * detail);`. Why it beats dots/haze: brightness comes from the CONTINUOUS field (no coverage trilemma ‚Äî see the dots note below), structure from the ridges (never foggy, never flat), and warm-dark dominance is AUTOMATIC ‚Äî everything under `thr` stays the void. Grade it with a `dotField` multiply + heavy `filmGrain` for recovered-footage texture (no moir√©-driving grid). Climax (doctrine 1): the filament CORES igniting ‚Äî a tightly-gated core term `smoothstep(0.72, 1.0, fire)`, CAPPED (`tone = min(tone, 0.90)`) so it never washes to flat cream, plus `bloom` on those hot cores. Do NOT drop `thr` enough to flood the frame ‚Äî that's the pale-wash blowout. Drive its evolution off the energy curve + drop: a churning network through the build, thinning to near-black embers in the breakdown, then flooding back with the cores flaring on the drop.
  - GLSL gotchas that cost rounds: don't reuse a header-injected name or re-declare one already in scope (e.g. a local `float t` after `t = u_time`) ‚Üí fragment-shader `redefinition` ‚Üí the ShaderLayer ERROR OVERLAY (tell: a ~1MB output file). Diagonalize a near-vertical advection vector to avoid vertical streaking.
  - **The atan branch-cut seam (polar / swirl / vortex fields).** `atan(y, x)` is DISCONTINUOUS: it jumps from +œÄ to ‚àíœÄ across the negative-x ray (the horizontal line running left from centre). Feed that raw angle straight into noise / warp / a texture lookup and the discontinuity prints as a HARD horizontal seam mid-frame ‚Äî a static defect stills rarely catch (it hides inside a busy swirl) but that the eye snags on in motion (it shipped once, `032.0.4L`). The angle input MUST be periodic: feed the noise a `sin(Œ∏), cos(Œ∏)` pair (continuous around the full turn), or wrap the angle to a period the noise shares (`Œ∏ * N / (2œÄ)` with an integer N so `+œÄ` and `‚àíœÄ` land on the same phase). Same trap for any `mod`/mirror/`polarFold` tiling boundary ‚Äî make the field agree across the seam. The metrics gate now WARNs on a sustained mid-frame row/column discontinuity (`seam.possible(y‚âà50%)`), but it's a backstop, not a licence: scrub the negative-x ray yourself.

## adopted techniques (vendored from Paper Shaders)

Eight primitives lifted from Paper Shaders (Apache-2.0, `github.com/paper-design/shaders`; their README explicitly permits use in videos) and retinted to canon. Each is a FIELD or MATERIAL ‚Äî route its output through `paletteRamp`/`paletteRampOk`, never ship Paper's own colours: keep the warm-dark ground dominant, Eclipse Gold as one accent when the material genuinely runs hot, cool hues as minor counter-accents only (the Retint Rule). Audio disturbs the MATERIAL in place ‚Äî amplitude, threshold, width, brightness ‚Äî on `u_audioHit`/`u_audioSwell`/the bands; the coordinate and time terms stay a constant clock (Motion law, doctrine 7).

- **`caustic(uv, t, scale, iterations)`** ‚Äî true water-caustic filaments (the 6-iteration rotate + sin/cos accumulation, squared): thin bright light-veins over a dark field, the light-through-water look. Retint low through `paletteRamp` so the filaments burn and the gaps stay Deep Field. Audio: drive `scale` and the output strength off `u_audioSwell` (the caustics tighten and brighten as the music surges); never put audio on `t`.
- **`neuroWeb(uv, t, iterations)`** ‚Äî zozuar's 15-iteration self-interfering sine web: a glowing organic filament mesh (neural, veined, mycelial). Square + `pow` it for contrast, then retint through `paletteRamp`. Audio: contrast on the hit (`u_audioHit` lifts the `pow` exponent / a harder threshold so the web crisps on the kick); brightness on `u_audioSwell`.
- **`swirlWarp(uv, t, swirl, iterations)`** ‚Äî an N-iteration sine swirl cascade returning a WARPED COORDINATE; a cheaper, more liquid `domainWarp`. Feed the result to any field (`fbm(swirlWarp(uv, u_time, s, 12) * 2.0, 5)`). Audio: the `swirl` amount rides a SMOOTHED band (`u_mid`/`u_audioSwell`) so the fabric folds harder IN PLACE; NEVER bend the `t` term (that translates the field ‚Üí reversal).
- **OKLab / `paletteRampOk(t)`** ‚Äî the perceptual sibling of `paletteRamp` over the same `u_palette` stops. OKLCH interpolation holds lightness+chroma and takes the short hue arc, so the red‚Üígold belt stays saturated instead of sagging into sRGB mud, and near-neutral darks (Deep Field) ramp cleanly with no spurious blue excursion. Drop it in wherever a ramp reads muddy; `paletteRamp` is untouched. `mixOklch(a, b, m)` mixes any two sRGB colours perceptually.
- **`colorSpots(uv, colors[6], count, t)`** ‚Äî inverse-distance-weighted (`1/d^3.5`) moving colour spots on per-index Lissajous paths (`colorSpotPosition`): a soft, always-moving smear of up to six colours. Pass RETINTED `colors` (canon stops or `paletteRamp` samples) so the smear stays on-brand. `firstFrameOffset`: seed `t` at a non-degenerate offset (`float t = 0.5 * (u_time + 41.5);`) so frame 0 isn't the collapsed pose where every spot sits on its Lissajous origin. `colorSpotSwirl(uv, amount)` adds an optional radius-scaled vortex. Audio: the spots already move on `t`; ride the swirl amount / a brightness gain on `u_audioSwell`.
- **`liquidMetal(uv, edge, repetition, shiftRed, shiftBlue, t, tint, tintA)`** ‚Äî Paper's procedural chrome: a stripe ramp bent by an edge-gradient bump, a per-channel R/B dispersion shift, and a color-burn tint, over an SDF-ish shape field `edge` (0..1) YOU supply (an `sdf` coverage, a `voronoi` wall, a filament field). Returns grey chrome ‚Äî retint through `retint()`/`paletteRamp`. Audio: the bump amount on `u_bassFast` (the metal swells and the highlight rolls under pressure). Skips Paper's image path (WebGL2-only `textureGrad`).

Two supporting techniques ship alongside:

- **`grainDisplace(uv, t, scale, amt)` ‚Äî grain that displaces the FIELD, not the pixels.** Returns a signed displacement you ADD to a shape / threshold / coordinate (`shape += grainDisplace(uv, u_time, 60.0, 0.06);`), so the colour BOUNDARIES themselves go grainy ‚Äî a rough, chewed edge instead of a clean vector line with grain merely laid over the fill. The recovered-footage grade where the emulsion ate the edge; pair it with `filmGrain` on the fill for a fully degraded plate.
- **exact `bayer2/bayer4/bayer8(coord)` ‚Äî crisp ordered dither.** The true printing matrices (WebGL1-safe mod-arithmetic; `step(bayer8(gl_FragCoord.xy), value)` to threshold), distinct from the header's `dither8` banding-killer and from `filmGrain`'s `grainBayer*` boil basis. `grainDither` gains a crisper basis if you trivially wire `bayer8` in (leave `grainFamilies` alone otherwise).

**tonemap ‚Äî cap the climax with `tonemap`, not `min()`.** When a hot core would blow past cream, don't hard-clip with `min(col, vec3(k))` ‚Äî that leaves a flat, clipped plateau. Roll the highlights off with a filmic soft-clip: `col = acesFilmic(col);` (or `reinhardJodie(col)`) saturates smoothly toward cream so the crest reads as burning, not blown. `liftGammaGain(col, lift, gamma, gain)` (scalar or vec3 args) is the lift/gamma/gain trim ‚Äî push the ground warm-dark and lift the mids before the ramp. Apply just before `dither8`.

## the third dimension: roil in place

The beat-pull gate punishes reversal, not evolution (motion doctrine above): a field that only translates glides but reads DEAD, and a curl-advected field ORBITS (returns ‚Üí reversal). The cure is a MONOTONIC third-dimension phase advance ‚Äî feed a field a coordinate whose z streams forward on `u_time`, so the forms roil and re-form IN PLACE as they stream, never returning. `GLSL.noise3` gives the 3D primitives ‚Äî `valueNoise3` / `fbm3` / `voronoi3` / `curl3` ‚Äî so the third axis IS the phase clock. Keep the z-advance SLOW and BROAD (fast/fine in-place churn flickers and bumps the gate).

```glsl
${GLSL.hash} ${GLSL.valueNoise} ${GLSL.fbm} ${GLSL.paletteRamp} ${GLSL.noise3}
// ... in main(), uv = gl_FragCoord.xy / u_res:
vec2 drift = vec2(0.13, 0.09) * (u_time * 0.06);   // constant one-way clock (global translation)
float z = u_time * 0.25;                            // monotonic phase advance (the third dimension)
float f = fbm3(vec3((uv + drift) * 3.0, z), 5);     // rivers roil in place as they stream
vec3 vor = voronoi3(vec3(uv * 4.0, z));             // cells re-form, never return
// audio drives WIDTH/THRESHOLD in place; z and drift stay constant clocks:
float thr = mix(0.06, 0.02, u_audioSwell);
float wall = smoothstep(thr, 0.0, vor.y - vor.x);
vec3 col = paletteRamp(mix(f, wall, 0.35));
```

`z` and `drift` advance on a constant clock; only the field AMPLITUDE / THRESHOLD / WIDTH take the audio. Forward-only evolution never returns ‚Üí no reversal ‚Üí the gate stays satisfied and the picture breathes. `curl3(p, t)` is the flowing counterpart: its z=t advance keeps the divergence-free flow forever fresh instead of scrolling a frozen 2D curl (dodging the curl-orbit trap in organic fields).

## smear: velocity written into the frame (the smear family)

The moodboard's smear region ‚Äî long-exposure / intentional-camera-movement, where motion ITSELF is the texture (the headlong, re-entry-velocity feel). No GLSL helper and no framework shortcut: `@remotion/motion-blur` (`Trail`/`CameraMotionBlur`) is DOM compositing that re-renders the whole subtree 10‚Äì50√ó per frame ‚Äî a crippling tax over a fullscreen shader. Do it IN-SHADER (~1√ó cost, full control):

- **Directional long-exposure.** Put your field in a function `vec3 field(vec2 uv, float t)`, then average a few taps stepped BACK IN TIME along the travel direction:
  ```glsl
  vec3 smear(vec2 uv) {
    vec3 acc = vec3(0.0); float wsum = 0.0;
    const int N = 6;
    for (int i = 0; i < N; i++) {
      float k = float(i) / float(N);                 // 0..1 back in time
      float w = 1.0 - k;                              // newer taps weigh more
      vec2 off = u_travelDir * k * u_smearLen;        // and back along the motion axis
      acc += field(uv - off, u_time - k * u_smearMs) * w;
      wsum += w;
    }
    return acc / wsum;
  }
  ```
  The field is a pure function of (uv, t), so sampling it at earlier t IS its past ‚Äî a true motion trail with no frame feedback (Remotion can't read frame N-1; this is the sanctioned way to fake trails).
- **Drive the smear length on a SMOOTHED signal** (Motion law): `u_smearMs`/`u_smearLen` rise with `u_audioSwell` / energy / the drop, so the frame streaks harder as the music surges and settles between ‚Äî velocity that breathes, never a per-kick snap. Keep the baseline tiny so calm passages stay legible.
- **Diagonalize** the travel vector off true-vertical/horizontal to avoid axis streaking artifacts.
- It doubles as a SMOOTHER: even a 2‚Äì3 tap temporal average softens the hard 30fps time-sampling on fast motion, reading more cinematic and organic than a naked frame ‚Äî reach for a light version on any fast vehicle, not only an explicit smear clip. (Don't push N high; it's per-pixel cost √óN.)

## raymarched 3D (volumetric)

For a volumetric or solid vehicle (a tumbling relic, a smoke volume, a metaball mass), `sdf3d` + `raymarch` give a 3D pipeline inside the fragment shader: define `float map(vec3 p)` from `sdSphere3` / `sdBox3` / `sdTorus3` (+ `smin` from `sdf` for smooth unions, `rot2` to tumble), then `raymarch(ro, rd, tmax)` and shade off `calcNormal`. For subject anatomy ‚Äî limbs, tapering spires, carved masonry, repeated colonnades ‚Äî reach for `GLSL.sdfPresence` (capsules, round cones, `smax` carving, limited repetition, the cheap 4-tap normal). Budget a march deliberately ‚Äî steps √ó map cost is THE knob, and ¬ßpresence staging carries the budget table, the low-step idiom (jittered start + short `tmax` + exponential fog), the `resolutionScale` lever, and the mandatory 10-frame timing probe. CRITICAL: a clean raymarched solid is the polished-CGI failure (SKILL.md) ‚Äî soak it in `filmGrain`, break the edges, and warp the surface with `fbm` / `domainWarp` so it reads as recovered footage, not a 3D render; presence renders cite this line as their treatment contract. Pairs naturally with `bloom` for an emissive core.

## bloom ‚Äî real emission glow (opt-in)

`<ShaderLayer bloom={{ threshold, intensity, radius }} />` adds a real multi-pass bloom: the bright pixels of YOUR shader are isolated, blurred at half-res, and added back, so hot material reads as genuinely luminous halation ‚Äî not a flat bright patch. Off unless you pass the prop (the render path is otherwise unchanged).

- **Bloom the vehicle's OWN hot material, never a glued-on orb (doctrine 1).** Bloom amplifies whatever is already bright, so make the bright thing the vehicle's crest / core / filaments going hot ‚Äî not a separate gradient circle. A clean bright disc plus bloom is still the banned fake-glow, just blurrier; texture the hot material (grain, broken edges) so the bloom reads as recovered-footage halation.
- **Tuning:** `threshold` (default 0.7) is the luminance cut ‚Äî raise it so only the true climax blooms; `intensity` (0.8) is how much is added back; `radius` (1.0) is the spread. Drive your shader's hot pixels with the drop envelope and the bloom blooms hardest exactly when the music peaks.
- **No feedback trails.** Bloom is single-frame. Cross-frame GPU feedback (motion echoes sampled from the previous frame) is NOT possible ‚Äî Remotion renders frames independently, so a shader can't read frame N-1. Fake trails with in-shader history instead (sample your field at a few `u_time - k` offsets and sum).

## palette discipline (paletteMix + the Retint Rule)

- Run `paletteMix(track.swatches)` to bend the artwork's colors toward the brand anchors; the roles are locked in code ‚Äî field stays Deep Field, the warm-dark ground holds, ink stays cream, artwork hues live only in the secondary swatches. A loud cover can never repaint the night: a cold blue sleeve must never extinguish the warm dark.
- The **Retint Rule** (MOODBOARD.md): steal the reference TECHNIQUE, retint everything to canon ‚Äî warm dark ground, Eclipse Gold as one accent among the scene's palette (never forced as a sun), Re-entry Red as the heat accent, Starlight Cream as ink. Cool hues survive only as minor counter-accents.
- In a shader, keep the `u_palette` / `paletteStops` on the canon ramp (Deep Field ‚Üí Re-entry Red ‚Üí Eclipse Gold ‚Üí Starlight Cream). Admit an artwork hue only as a separate minor seep uniform, never as a ramp stop. Gold is an accent the scene may reach when its material runs hot, not a mandatory sun.
- **WHEN a warm scene's vehicle does run gold: MIX, don't ADD.** Additively blending Eclipse Gold over a warm orange/red field sums into a green/lime smear. So when the climax genuinely calls for gold on a warm ground, `mix()`/lerp the colour toward a locked gold stop (optionally darkening the bed locally under the crest first) so it reads as true gold, not lime. Reserve additive blending for gold over a dark or cool ground. (This is conditional ‚Äî a cool or non-gold scene climaxes in its own colour and never reaches for gold at all; doctrine 1.)

## fractal / glass

These are good diversity lanes because they avoid the local-history loop (doctrine 3).

- **fractal:** `GLSL.polarFold(uv, segments)` folds the frame into N mirrored wedges; fly inward by scaling the folded coords with `u_time`/`arc` and tint the rings from the artwork accent. The climax is the fold center intensifying in the scene's palette ‚Äî a hot core whose colour is the scene's (gold only when that core genuinely runs hot, never reached for by default).
- **glass:** refractive vertical blades sweeping across the frame ‚Äî per-blade UV refraction offsets that breathe with `u_bass`, specular brightness on `u_energy`, the sweep position travelling on the arc. The climax is one specular bulge surging ‚Äî its colour the scene's own (a steel/teal/blue surge for a cool scene; gold only when the blade's own highlight genuinely runs hot).

## the two registers: fully abstract OR recognizably real (never the middle)

The whole-frame law (SKILL.md) gives you exactly two registers; pick ONE per render and make the whole canvas commit to it. The failure is the **uncanny middle** ‚Äî a half-real shape that reads as neither (blobs-with-lines that look like nothing).

- **Fully abstract** ‚Äî the texture/field IS the image, edge to edge: an fbm/curl haze, a voronoi cell-wall screen, a caustic field, a dense stipple swarm that owns the canvas. No subject; the surface is alive and that is enough. (This is what `sonnet-plain`'s vein-screen got right ‚Äî the field WAS the frame.)
- **Recognizably real, shown abstractly ‚Äî nameable AND PLACED (SKILL.md, redefined 2026-07-04).** The eye must NAME it and PLACE it: the subject has a spatial relation to the observer ‚Äî scale, distance, a viewpoint, occlusion, a ground or horizon it sits in. This only works if you build on the subject's ACTUAL structure, not a vague impression of it: a real murmuration is streaming rivers with dark lanes and frayed edges (not a fuzzy cloud); real flowers are petals with form (see the moodboard's `halftone-tulip-bloom.png`, `crt-scanline-roses.png`, `dreamy-blurred-wildflowers.png` ‚Äî real flora, abstracted but legible); a wing has ordered veins; a hull has one coherent mass. A texture that merely RESEMBLES a material (basalt, strata, an echogram) is abstract with a caption ‚Äî declare it abstract. The check is the stranger test: on the shipped poster, a stranger says "there is a THING there," names it as a kind, and says where it sits. If the subject slides toward the uncanny middle, retreat presence-first: distance + silhouette, then deeper treatment, and only then fully abstract (SKILL.md).

When you DO go representational, two modes exist and the stranger test's PLACE half decides which you are in. The three treatments below are the **image-plane mode** ‚Äî they abstract a FLAT subject (subject-as-texture) and keep it sharp, legible, and beat-syncing more easily than soft haze (so doctrine 9 comes for free). A subject WITH PRESENCE ‚Äî placed in space, at a distance, under a viewpoint ‚Äî is staged through **¬ßpresence staging** below instead. Three image-plane reference treatments (MOODBOARD.md):

- **Halftone dot-screen** (`halftone-tulip-bloom.png`) ‚Äî quantise a soft form (a bloom, a body, the energy field) through a dot grid whose dot RADIUS rides the beat. The dots are sharp; the subject reads through them.
- **CRT / scanline raster** (`crt-scanline-roses.png`) ‚Äî horizontal scanlines + an RGB cell grid over a subject; jitter/roll the lines on onsets, swell the phosphor bloom on energy. Degraded broadcast, not clean UI.
- **Contour-line displacement** (`contour-eclipse-lines.png`) ‚Äî the Unknown-Pleasures motif used REPRESENTATIONALLY: a field of horizontal lines that DISPLACE around hidden forms so an eclipse/figure emerges from the warp (flat sparse lines that reveal nothing are the failure to avoid). Drive the displacement amplitude from the energy/bass curves; the climax is a single crest line intensifying in the scene's palette.

**Vary the axis and the primitive.** Those three references all happen to be horizontal-line / raster motifs ‚Äî left unchecked, agents converge on "horizontal lines + a horizontal gold bar." The register is far wider; reach past lines for: a **dot / stipple screen** (halftone as DOTS, or a particle/ember field quantised to a grid, dot radius riding the beat), a **woven mesh or moir√©** interference, a **cellular / voronoi crackle** (cracked glaze, a dry lakebed, cell walls breathing on the bass), **caustics / refraction** (light bent through water or glass, the hot focus swelling on energy), an **emulsion / chemical bloom**. Pick a structure whose axis and primitive differ from the recent archive (read the vehicle ledger via `fluncle admin tracks vehicles --json`), and let the climax take the form that structure implies ‚Äî a brightening cell, a caustic hot-spot, a sweeping shimmer, a single dot that blooms ‚Äî in the scene's own colour, **not always a horizontal bar, not a default circular sun, and not forced to gold** (doctrine 1: one committed climax, free form and free colour).

Keep the warm dark throughout ‚Äî the structure is the vehicle, not a second light source. Any gold is one accent among the scene's palette, present only when the vehicle's own material genuinely runs hot, never bolted on as a sun.

## presence staging: subjects with presence (placed, indifferent, resolved)

The staging grammar for a subject with presence ‚Äî a passing hull, alien flora, a ruin, a distant creature, a terrain ‚Äî abstract in TREATMENT (the layer-split law, SKILL.md), PLACED in space, strictly INDIFFERENT to the music (the three-clocks law, doctrine 7), FELT from frame one and RESOLVED across the arc (doctrine 2). Everything here is one fullscreen fragment pass on the existing kit; the raymarch contract above holds throughout (a clean solid is the polished-CGI failure ‚Äî soak it, break it, fog it), and the target sits on the legibility ladder at rung 2‚Äì3 presences behind a rung-4 anchor: nameable as a KIND, alien in every detail.

### the soul law: treatment perceptible at viewing size, EVERYWHERE (the operator's highest ruling, 2026-07-04)

The layer-split law tells you WHICH layer to abuse; this tells you HOW MUCH, and it is the top ruling of the whole direction ‚Äî it overrides a clean gate. **The treatment must be PERCEPTIBLE AT VIEWING SIZE, in every region of every frame.** Two checks make it mechanical. **The Keynote test:** every region reads as MATERIAL, never as a smooth analytic gradient you could draw with three colour stops and a `mix()`. The sky carries structure (dither, drifting motes, a faint low-amplitude field); the fog is material with visible MULTI-OCTAVE wisps (`fbm`/`curlNoise`, so the density has texture, not a linear falloff); every glow carries dirt (`GLSL.glowWithDirt` ‚Äî light through a dirty medium). If a region would survive being redrawn as a clean ramp, it is under-treated ‚Äî put the medium back into it. **The phone-size check:** downscale a still to ~400px wide ‚Äî the actual feed surface ‚Äî and the medium's fingerprints (grain clump, dither pitch, chroma-bleed, the boil) must STILL be visible there. If they vanish at 400px, the kernel is too small or the amount too low: coarsen the grain, widen the dither pitch, and lift the amount until moodboard-level grain/dirt survives the downscale. The five moodboard treatment references ARE the bar (`crt-cosmos-bloom-coral.png`, `dither-hourglass-glitch.png`, `dreamy-blurred-wildflowers.png`, `grain-pink-carnival-mask-bloom.png`, `waveform-ridge.png`) ‚Äî match their perceptible density, not a fainter memory of it. **And the AUTHORED SILHOUETTE carries the alien-ness:** articulate the subject's outline AT FEATURE SCALE ‚Äî notches, damage, asymmetry, repetition-with-damage (a ruined colonnade whose columns break and lean, a hull with snapped spars and uneven ports, a growth that repeats then fractures) ‚Äî so the eye reads a specific, weathered, alien THING. Spend the silhouette budget on damage and asymmetry the way you spend the surface budget on grain; a clean rectangle (or any un-notched primitive) reads as cardboard, and the alien-ness of the whole page rides on that outline being genuinely articulated.

### the five staging patterns

- **Horizon + atmospheric perspective (the "this is a SPACE" cue).** A depth-keyed fog ramp where contrast, saturation, AND edge sharpness all collapse together with depth toward a warm-dark atmosphere stop: `col = mix(col, mix(u_palette[1], u_palette[2], 0.2), 1.0 - exp(-depth * k))` ‚Äî the fog colour is the scene's own dark air, never a default blue (Retint). Atmospheric perspective is the one depth cue that also encodes SIZE: a silhouette at 20% contrast that still fills half the frame is necessarily colossal. Let a height-fog term eat the subject's BASE (`fogAmt *= exp(-(p.y - groundY) * k)`): an object whose contact points you cannot see stays unresolved, so the mind keeps rendering it. Audio: the bass PRESSURIZES the air ‚Äî `density ‚Üê u_bass`, in place ‚Äî and you declare that as the FIRST structural binding in the intent, so the coupling headline correlates against the curve that actually drives the picture.
- **Silhouette-over-live-field (the inverted treatment).** A matte dark mass (2D SDF or a marched body) OCCLUDING a reactive field. Occlusion is the cheapest, strongest presence cue there is ‚Äî and the treatment inverts: ALL of it goes to the WORLD (the field behind gets the haze, echo, and boil), while the subject carries only a rim term on its light-facing edge plus the global grain. The eye reads the untouched dark shape against the treated world as nearer and solid, and the matte interior is the uncanny-middle insurance ‚Äî a silhouette demands no interior detail to get wrong.
- **The scale ladder (parallax + a sharp datum).** 2‚Äì3 painter-ordered layers of form at different scales and drift rates ‚Äî near: sharp, high-frequency, fast parallax; far: soft, low-frequency, near-still (a spatial-frequency ratio near 10:1 and a parallax ratio near 15:1 read as VAST). The near layer IS the anchor rule's sharp datum (a bristled stalk, spars, a rigging line ‚Äî rung 4+): it calibrates the whole frame and licenses the far presences to melt. Keep the foreground reactive (it is the observer's side of the glass) and the background indifferent.
- **The constant-clock passage (duration-as-size).** A body wider than the frame by 3‚Äì5√ó, translating one-way on pure `u_time`, crossing a fraction of its own length over the whole clip ‚Äî constant velocity, never modulated (indifference is constant velocity; that is the physics of mass). Advance the surface-pattern phase monotonically so the texture demonstrably continues past both frame edges. End the clip MID-PASS: the off-frame continuation outlives the clip into the `CloseCard`, and the pass never completes (the presence killers, SKILL.md).
- **The swell/drop reveal (resolution, never introduction).** The subject is perceptible from frame one ‚Äî a darker-dark, a coarse dither ghost, a silhouette the fog wraps (presence ‚âÝ disclosure, doctrine 2). Fog transmittance falls slowly on `u_audioSwell` through the build; THE reveal rides the existing `u_audioDrop` envelope (the 700ms rise is flash-safe by construction): transmittance drops hard + the key light rises + the dither resolution snaps finer ‚Äî ONCE, on the drop, never per beat ‚Äî and HOLDS through the outro (one rising transition that holds is zero flash pairs), then the veil re-swallows the glimpse. Arc discipline: the occluded front half must itself evolve ‚Äî fog banks drifting with parallax, shafts sweeping ‚Äî or the whole-clip arc mean dips under the floor even with a spectacular reveal; a dim freeze waiting for the drop fails BOTH doctrine 2 and the arc gate. The parting fog swings luminance AND exposes new edges at once, so the intent drop-spike check passes on both channels.
  - **The drop-less variant (liquid rollers ‚Äî `audio.dropMs` null).** When the analyzer finds NO drop (a continuous roller with no breakdown‚Üíslam, so `audio.dropMs` is null and there is no `u_audioDrop` crest to ride), the reveal rides the DISSOLVE instead: resolution keyed to FALLING bass (`1.0 - u_bass` across a phrase, so the veil thins as the low end drains at a bar boundary) OR a MANUALLY-placed swell crest ‚Äî set `reactivity.drop.peakTimeMs` to a chosen `audio.dropCandidates` moment or a bar you pick off `audio.downbeats`, which synthesizes a `u_audioDrop` envelope at that instant. Declare the disclosure accordingly in the intent: set `dropMs` to the crest you chose (so the arc drop-spike check reads the right window), or declare the disclosure as `revealing` riding the dissolve. Everything else holds ‚Äî one smooth rising resolution that HOLDS, never a per-beat pump.

### the arc-vs-flash tension (earn the arc with a slow surge, not a bright pulse)

Two HARD gates pull in OPPOSITE directions on a presence reveal, and the pilot lost six render cycles rediscovering it ‚Äî so state it outright. The ARC floor wants a large LUMINANCE reorganization across the clip (the picture must visibly become something else, or it reads dead). The FLASH gate punishes a large-AREA, high-magnitude luminance oscillation on the per-beat clock (a bright field pumped by the bass strobes a 10¬∞ sub-window and fails). The two reconcile exactly ONE way: **earn the arc with a SLOW, SMOOTH reveal SURGE** ‚Äî drive the whole-frame brightness/transmittance shift off the SMOOTHED envelopes (`u_audioSwell` through the build, `u_audioDrop`'s 700ms rise at the climax, or the drop-less dissolve above), as ONE rising transition that HOLDS, never a bright field re-modulated by the raw `u_bass` every beat. A large field pumping on the bass is the exact fixture the flash gate exists to catch; a field that never reorganizes is the exact fixture the arc gate exists to catch ‚Äî and a bright-per-beat field manages to be BOTH. Keep the ONE mandatory fast accent (the `u_kickHit`/`u_snareHit` beat-grid answer, doctrine 9) SMALL IN AREA ‚Äî a mote burst, a rim tick, a wake ‚Äî so it satisfies the beat-grid check without ever painting a 10¬∞ window. Big + slow + smooth carries the arc; small + fast carries the accent; there is no bright-per-beat middle that clears both gates.

### the witness layer (diegetic instrumentation)

Presence doubles when the frame carries evidence that an INSTRUMENT saw the subject: crop-mark brackets, dotted leader lines converging on the form, a small numeral, one hot lens streak, scanner dust, registration marks. The rule that makes it work: the instrument layer is CRISP while the world is dissolved ‚Äî sharp 1px brackets over a dithered relic read as "measured, not drawn." Keep it sparse and plausible (a few marks, never a texture of marks), keep it diegetic (the recovered-footage fiction's own survey apparatus, not UI chrome), and keep the facts law intact: any numeral is scene-derived from fields the props expose (the Log ID digits, the BPM, the year ‚Äî Oxanium is sanctioned for numerals), never an invented figure, and the `TypePlate` still owns the actual facts layout (doctrine 4‚Äì5).

### glow-with-dirt (every additive light)

Any additive light ‚Äî a corner glow, a shaft, a portal edge, the climax ignition ‚Äî carries occluding dark speckle whose density is PROPORTIONAL to luminance: light through a dirty physical medium. The habit is "grain hides in shadows"; recovered exposure puts the dirt IN the light, and that inversion is the single strongest "scanned, not computed" cue in the moodboard corpus. `GLSL.glowWithDirt` packages the idiom:

```glsl
float g = glowTerm(uv);                                 // your additive light term
float n = clumpyNoise(uv * S + boilSeed);               // clump ~0.002 fw, boiling per frame
col += g * ramp;
col -= g * 0.25 * step(0.82, n);                        // the dirt rides the light
```

plus, optionally, a faint 3-tap one-way echo of the glow alone. The speckle boils at the grain family's own rate (never crawled). This is the cheapest single upgrade to ANY composition, presence render or not ‚Äî it converts "additive shader glow" into "recovered exposure" for a handful of lines; reach for it on every light source.

### the monotonic retint (`rampRetint`)

`GLSL.rampRetint` is the gradient-map retint: grayscale luma through a 4‚Äì6 stop hue ramp that is MONOTONIC IN LUMA. Hue may go anywhere the Retint Rule allows; brightness order never reorders ‚Äî and that property alone is why a subject survives the most violent recolour (the layer-split law: luma ordering is a name-carrier). Use it to steal a reference's colour treatment wholesale without touching the subject's shading; soft-posterize on top (~6 levels, mixed 70/30 with the smooth ramp) for the print/silkscreen feel without hard banding.

### worlds that remember (`useAccumulated`)

Cross-frame GPU feedback stays foreclosed (the bloom section) ‚Äî but every audio curve is known ahead of render, so a leaky integrator over a curve is computable EXACTLY per frame on the CPU and pushed as a uniform. `useAccumulated(curve, decay)` gives real hysteresis with zero GPU state and full determinism: scorch that accumulates across the drop, a colonnade that stays lit after the climax, heat that drains slowly through the outro. A world that remembers the drop is a world, not a filter.

### the render budget (marched presence scenes)

A raymarch costs steps √ó map, and that product is THE budget knob. Plan in noise-eq (1 noise-eq ‚âà one `valueNoise` call; a typical shipped field shader lands at ~30‚Äì60 noise-eq/px ‚âà the current 30‚Äì60 min box render), so: **wall-clock ‚âà (noise-eq √∑ 40) √ó the current render time.** Realistic budgets for a 20s clip on the box at `resolutionScale: 0.5` (roughly double them at full res):

| marcher                                          | steps √ó map | ‚âà box wall-clock |
| ------------------------------------------------ | ----------- | ---------------- |
| analytic solid (‚â§10 prims + smin), fog, jittered | 32 √ó 5      | ~45‚Äì90 min       |
| + shading-time fbm bump (2‚Äì3 taps at the hit)    | +20         | ~1h              |
| displaced organic (map + 2-octave `fbm3`)        | 40 √ó 8      | ~1.5‚Äì2h          |
| heightfield fixed-step terrain (3-octave fbm)    | 50 √ó 7      | ~1.5‚Äì2.5h        |
| volumetric density march (2-octave)              | 36 √ó 8      | ~1.5‚Äì2h          |

The low-step idiom that makes 24‚Äì40 steps look intentional: (1) **jitter the ray start** per pixel with interleaved-gradient noise (`ign`, in `GLSL.sdfPresence`) so undersampling artifacts become NOISE the Light-Years grain legitimately eats ‚Äî the brand's mandatory degradation IS the forgiveness mechanism, the deep synergy of this whole direction; (2) **cap `tmax` SHORT** and let exponential fog (`1.0 - exp(-k*t)`) swallow everything beyond it ‚Äî a missed surface in fog is invisible; (3) **shade cheap** ‚Äî keep noise OUT of `map()` (bump the normal with 2‚Äì3 fbm taps at the hit instead of displacing the field), drop fbm octaves with distance, and prefer the 4-tap tetrahedral `calcNormal4` (or normal-less rim + one-direction relief) over the 6-tap normal. `ShaderLayer`'s `resolutionScale` is the single biggest lever (√∑4 fragment cost at 0.5) and the upscale softness reads as the brand's degraded glass ‚Äî default it on for any marched hero. **The 10-frame timing probe (run it before every full marched render):** render 10 frames on the box with `FLUNCLE_GL=swangle`, time them, extrapolate √ó60 ‚Äî rebudget any concept projecting past ~3h unless the report deliberately flags it. **And fog-soften every marched silhouette:** never `step()` on hit/miss ‚Äî blend by distance-to-surface at ray exit. That is both the look (no CGI edge) AND the determinism guard: the preview runs ANGLE/Metal and the ship render runs SwiftShader, and a ray that GRAZES a silhouette can hit on one renderer and miss on the other ‚Äî a binary per-pixel divergence a fog blend erases. Judge a marched scene on the box render, never on preview pixels.

## Framing devices: how the finding is HELD (the log feel)

The moodboard names three ways to HOLD the finding that cut across texture families ‚Äî reach for one deliberately when the concept wants "a thing observed/held," to deepen the diary register (the soul) and break the centred-orb default:

- **portal / threshold** ‚Äî a framed window onto the warm side standing inside the dark (a mirror propped in foliage, a receding doorway, a dye-bleed aperture). ### Density: dots, particles, flocks (the sparse-flock fix)

A dot/stipple/particle/swarm vehicle lives or dies on COUNT. The recurring miss: an agent hand-rolls a coarse one-dot-per-cell grid at ~80‚Äì120 cells, which is only a few hundred dots once gated to a body ‚Äî so a "vast murmuration" renders as scattered confetti, the **flat-chart failure** (SKILL.md). The concept it was chasing has THOUSANDS of points. The canvas renders at full 1080√ó1920 and a cell grid costs the same per pixel no matter how many cells, so density is free ‚Äî there is no reason to come out sparse.

Use **`GLSL.dotField`** and think in HUNDREDS of cells:

```glsl
${GLSL.hash} ${GLSL.fbm} ${GLSL.dotField}
// ... in main(), after you have a 0..1 `body` density field (fbm membrane, SDF,
// energy field ‚Äî the shape the flock occupies) and a per-pixel cell id:
float dots = max(
  dotField(uv, u_res, 240.0, 0.0022, 0.7, u_seed),        // base octave
  dotField(uv, u_res, 360.0, 0.0016, 0.8, u_seed + 19.0)  // finer octave ‚Üí sub-grid density
);
// gate the dots to the BODY so they form an organism, not confetti:
float present = step(hash21(floor(uv * u_res / 6.0)), smoothstep(0.15, 0.6, body));
dots *= present * smoothstep(0.1, 0.5, body);
```

- **Count is the cell number, not the radius.** 240‚Äì360 cells across the height gives thousands of legible points; ~100 reads as static. Keep the dot `radius` ‚â• ~0.0015 (‚âà3px) and AA'd (the helper does this) or h264 eats the fine ones.
- **Body-gate every dot.** Multiply by a density field + a membership `step()` so the dots have a shape with edges and depth ‚Äî never an even scatter across the frame (that's the formless/confetti failure).
- **Reactivity rides the dots' MATERIAL** per the Motion law (doctrine 7): dot radius swells on the hit, the membership threshold / glow lifts on the energy swell, grain kicks on onsets. What audio may do to the flock's MOTION is governed by doctrine 7 ‚Äî read it and stay inside it.
- For a true sense of a living mass, layer 2‚Äì3 octaves at different cell counts and let the densest core read as depth (a faint diffuse haze of the body underneath the dots sells "one organism," not loose points).
- **`dotField` is best as a TEXTURE OVERLAY, not the lead.** As the PRIMARY image, dots hit a trilemma you cannot tune out: big dots merge into smooth silk ribbons (the clean look the brand rejects); small dots drop coverage and go dark/flat; uniform coverage reads flat; and the regular grid throws curving MOIR√â over any smooth brightness gradient. The fix isn't a better cell count ‚Äî it's role. When you just want "dense and alive," make the STRUCTURE a continuous field (ridged filaments, a curl/voronoi/caustic screen ‚Äî see organic fields) and let `dotField` + grain ride ON it as the recovered-footage grade. The sparse-flock advice above holds for the NARROWER case where a literal stipple SWARM genuinely IS the subject (a murmuration, an ember field) ‚Äî there, count is king. Reaching for a dot field as the lead because "dense" is what produced a mild, silky first attempt; lead with the field, grade with the dots.
