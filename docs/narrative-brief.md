# Fluncle Narrative Canon

## Overview

Fluncle is not a music platform.

Fluncle is not a playlist.

Fluncle is not a social media account.

Fluncle is a cosmonaut.

An explorer.

A traveler.

A collector of observations from distant places.

The internet-facing manifestation of Fluncle is a logbook.

Every track added to Fluncle becomes a log entry.

These entries are spread across the internet:

- Website
- TikTok
- RSS
- Discord
- CLI
- SSH
- Future mediums

Together they tell the story of a traveler moving through time, space, dimensions, and parallel worlds.

Music is the artifact attached to each observation.

The log itself is the primary object.

The track is evidence.

---

# Core Concept

Fluncle travels through reality.

At each stop, Fluncle records observations.

These observations become logs.

A log may contain:

- a song
- an image
- a note
- a transmission
- a visual artifact
- a memory

Music is simply one of the ways Fluncle experiences the universe.

---

# The Logbook

The logbook is the central artifact.

Everything in Fluncle revolves around logs.

Every log has a stable identifier.

Example:

txt fluncle://241.7.3A

This identifier represents a unique observation.

The identifier is permanent.

The same log can be referenced from:

txt TikTok Website CLI SSH RSS Discord

without changing.

---

# Log Identifiers

Identifiers resemble:

txt 241.7.3A 773.2.9B 1041.4.1C

The format intentionally resembles:

- dates
- coordinates
- version numbers
- IP addresses
- stardates

while being none of them.

The exact meaning should remain partially mysterious.

The identifier should feel like something recovered rather than generated.

Users should understand:

> This is a point in a larger journey.

without necessarily understanding how the identifier works.

---

# Canonical URI

The canonical identifier format is:

txt fluncle://241.7.3A

This is the true identity of a log.

Everything else is a representation.

Examples:

txt fluncle://241.7.3A

↓

txt https://fluncle.com/log/241.7.3A

↓

bash fluncle open 241.7.3A

↓

bash ssh logs.fluncle.com 241.7.3A

All refer to the same observation.

---

# Public Language

Fluncle should avoid modern social media language.

Avoid:

txt Post Content Article Upload Playlist

Prefer:

txt Log Observation Transmission Signal Artifact Recovery Archive Entry Sector Anomaly

Examples:

txt New transmission recovered.

txt Observation logged.

txt Archive updated.

txt Recovered artifact attached.

---

# Relationship Between Logs And Music

A log is not a song.

A log may contain a song.

Example:

txt LOG 241.7.3A Sector: A3-77 Recovered Artifact: Delta Heavy — Ecstasy Observation: Local inhabitants exhibit unusual kinetic behavior. Temporal stability remains uncertain.

The music serves as evidence that the observation occurred.

The music is not the observation itself.

---

# Tone Of Voice

Fluncle is curious.

Never cynical.

Never aggressive.

Never corporate.

Fluncle observes rather than judges.

The tone should feel:

- exploratory
- scientific
- lonely
- curious
- slightly mysterious
- occasionally humorous

Examples:

txt Arrived in an unfamiliar sector. Local signals exhibit unusual rhythmic properties.

txt Temporal distortion detected. Further study recommended.

txt An unexpectedly energetic civilization was encountered.

---

# Website

The website should be treated as an archive.

Not a feed.

Not a blog.

Not a social platform.

Users browse logs.

Examples:

txt Archive Recent Logs 241.7.3A 241.7.4A 241.8.1B

A log page:

txt LOG 241.7.3A Observation Recovered Artifact Published Transmissions Related Logs

---

# SSH Interface

SSH represents direct access to the archive.

Example:

bash ssh logs.fluncle.com

Returns:

txt FLUNCLE ARCHIVE Recent Logs 241.7.3A 241.7.4A 241.8.1B >

Viewing a log:

bash ssh logs.fluncle.com 241.7.3A

Returns:

txt LOG 241.7.3A Recovered Artifact: Delta Heavy — Ecstasy Observation: ...

The SSH interface should feel like a recovered terminal from a distant research vessel.

---

# TikTok

TikTok videos are visualized log entries.

The identifier should appear visibly.

Example:

txt LOG 241.7.3A

or:

txt fluncle://241.7.3A

The identifier should appear:

- in the video
- in the caption
- in the website URL

This allows users to follow the trail.

---

# RSS

RSS is the transmission feed.

Each item represents a recovered log.

Example:

xml <guid>fluncle://241.7.3A</guid>

The feed is not a podcast feed.

It is a stream of observations.

---

# CLI

CLI users interact directly with the archive.

Examples:

bash fluncle log 241.7.3A

bash fluncle latest

bash fluncle archive

The CLI should reinforce the fiction that users are exploring a logbook rather than querying a database.

---

# Long-Term Vision

Over time, a user should be able to encounter:

txt 241.7.3A

on TikTok.

Then discover:

txt 241.7.3A

on the website.

Then discover:

txt 241.7.3A

through RSS.

Then discover:

txt 241.7.3A

through SSH.

Eventually realizing:

> These are not social media posts.

> These are logs from a much larger journey.

The archive should feel like a living record of a traveler exploring the universe through music.

The internet is simply where the logbook is stored.
