# RFC: Fluncle Foundry — a finding's life, made legible

**Status:** Final (research → /taste → 4-role adversarial panel synthesized, 2026-06-26) — completeness standard applied.
**For:** a fresh build session (or a small fleet), executing the art-first rebuild of the `/factory` page in gates.
**Canon/authority:** `DESIGN.md`, `VOICE.md` + the `copywriting-fluncle` skill, `PRODUCT.md` arbitrate (this is planning, not spec). Source vision: `docs/ROADMAP.md` "From Earth to Orbit". The codebase wins on any conflict.

> Process note: divergent research across five threads (data wiring · AI-art generation · render engineering · canon/palette/voice · product/scope), a /taste pass, and a 4-role adversarial panel (staff engineer · design-brand · data/realtime · product-scope) that verified claims live. Their corrections **substantially reshaped this RFC** — from "rewrite `/factory` into a full-screen tableau" to "art-first, gated, diegetic, finding-as-hero." Verifications + sources in the appendix.

## The standard (definition of done)

The whole thing, done right, tests + docs included — "holy shit, that's done." But the panel's central finding is that "the whole thing" was mis-scoped: a render-engine rewrite was bundled with the art drop-in as if inseparable, and they are not. So completeness here means **a complete art-first delivery in gates**, where each gate ships something whole and the next earns itself:

- **Nothing achievable is cut** — but the order is real. **Gate A** (the honesty fixes + the teal retint + the finding-as-hero canon pass + the full machine art, all on v1's _existing, proven_ side-scroll) is a complete, shippable win that stands even if everything after it stalls. **Gate B** (the bigger build) is a complete delivery that must _earn itself_ against a one-machine art pilot and two vision decisions only Maurice can make.
- **Tests + docs are part of done:** add `sim.test.ts` before generalizing the sim; add a `stationOf`↔SQL agreement test if the aggregate ships; update `docs/factory.md`; document the new `generate_scene.py` path in the `fluncle-sprites` skill.
- **The only sanctioned "not now":** the genuinely later arc phases the brief walls off (per-finding unique sprites, the collectable binder, public accounts), and the two honest external dependencies — Maurice's art taste-loop, and the two vision calls (find-vs-make, scroll-vs-painting) that gate Gate B.
- **Dangling threads this ties off:** the documented-but-unwired `/factory/<id>.png` swap loader (it never existed — Gate 0 builds it); the faked readouts (`RENDERING 42%`, `GENERATING SCRIPT`, `QA PASS`, uptime, countdowns) that are fiction in **v1 today**; and a real data bug the panel surfaced — the newest-48 poll window **truncates the oldest stuck findings**, i.e. the deep backlog the product exists to show.

## 0. Summary — the reframe (corrected by the panel)

**The unifying simplification: make the invisible lifecycle legible _diegetically_ — you READ state from the scene, not from counters — with the FINDING as the one lit hero moving through warm-dark apparatus.** Three moves, each resolving a canon break the first draft walked past:

1. **Finding-as-hero, not machine-as-hero.** The reference makes ~18 glorious brass machines the spectacle and the covers small cargo — a straight inversion of the founding rule (PRODUCT: _"the cover art is the founding document… the music leads the eye… the story it tells is the point"_). Fix: the machines **recede to warm-dark silhouette**; the single Eclipse-Gold **One Sun heats the active finding's cover**, which blooms to a readable hero (its Log ID legible) at the working machine. One sun, tracking the banger through the works. This one move reconciles **cover-led + One Sun (singular, ≤10%) + "one thing at a time" + the find-not-make persona** at once.
2. **Diegetic, not dashboard.** The reference's `FOUNDRY STATUS` panel (UPTIME / PROCESSED TODAY / ACTIVE TRACKS / SYSTEMS OPERATIONAL) is a **literal violation of a named rule** — DESIGN: _"no heroes, **no metrics**, no pitch; the archive IS the page"_; PRODUCT bans _"SaaS dashboards"_ by name. An _honest_ dashboard is still a dashboard. **Cut the panel.** The canon already gives the substitute: the **pile of covers in front of the slow machine IS the queue depth**; three covers staged at the pad ARE "ready to launch"; a cover launching IS "shipped." Counters are the last resort for the few facts the scene genuinely cannot show (the archive's age), and live in an `sr-only`/noscript affordance or a single quiet plate — never a HUD.
3. **Renderer of true state, or it isn't Fluncle.** `RENDERING 42%`, `GENERATING SCRIPT…`, `QA PASS`, `FOUNDRY UPTIME 14D`, and the launch countdown are **fiction** — the system computes none of them. Each is demoted to a truthful equivalent (binary state, the resting "waiting for its note" line, the real launch gate, `min(added_at)` as the line's real age, event-driven liftoff). This is the brief's whole thesis, and these fakes exist in **v1 today** — so the demotion table (§3.B) is a standalone fix worth shipping regardless of the rest.

**Strategy: art-first and gated.** The panel verified that the only hard dependency to "detailed, alive machines" is the unwired **~15-line PNG-swap loader** + the AI-art loop — _not_ a render-engine rewrite. So:

- **Gate 0 (de-risk):** generate ONE detailed canon machine, wire the loader, drop it onto **v1 as-is**, view it live. Prove it reads as steel-not-clay, as one family, placed-not-pasted, before committing to ~8 assets (the historical attractor-convergence risk) or any rewrite.
- **Gate A (ships on v1, graceful floor):** the honesty demotions + the teal-phosphor retint + the finding-as-hero canon pass + the full machine-art set, on the **existing side-scroll**. Delivers "detailed, alive, canon, finding-as-hero on the proven belt" with no rewrite, no new geometry, no endpoint — and fails gracefully (if the art stalls, v1 still stands).
- **Gate B (earns itself):** the bigger build — _only_ after Gate 0 clears Maurice's eye **and** he makes the two vision calls below. This is where a fixed everything-visible tableau (or a richer scroll), the layout/render rewrite, the deep-backlog fix, and any surviving aggregate live.

**Keep the spine — but honestly.** Keep `stations.ts` (the one genuinely tested file), `sim.ts`'s token/queue/launch logic (scalar-position-agnostic, so the belt-shape change is bounded), the route's data layer, `grain.ts`, `audio.ts`. Correction the panel forced: the DOM-led architecture pushes plates + readouts into **React** (derived straight from the poll via `stationOf`, already imported in `factory.tsx`), so the canvas engine _shrinks_ and `factory.tsx` _grows_ — "factory.tsx barely changes" was wrong.

## 1. Context & goals

v1 `/factory` shipped scruffy-but-real (the brief's bar: prove the sync, then iterate hard on feel). It works but reads thin. Maurice's reference ("FLUNCLE FOUNDRY") sets the aesthetic target — detailed, brass-and-brick, alive, retinted to canon. This RFC is that iterate-hard pass, scoped honestly after the panel cut the over-build.

**In reach this build:** detailed AI machines retinted to canon, wired to real per-finding state; the finding-as-hero lit-cover treatment; honest readouts (no fakes); the cover-pile-as-queue diegetic backlog; the launch→`/galaxy` cinematic; full a11y + reduced-motion + noscript; launch-gated like `/earth`.

**Honest horizon (outside our control):** the _art quality_ gates on Maurice's taste loop (iterative, "a lot of it"); sub-second realtime is **not** a goal (the underlying events are cron-driven, minutes apart — a 15–30s poll already samples 20–240× faster than the fastest real event). And two **vision** calls gate Gate B (below).

**Through-line:** Earth = the ground, the **Foundry = the works between**, the Galaxy = the sky. The launch valve is the same event as Earth's rocket→`/galaxy`; mirror its cinematic.

## 2. The decisions that gate everything

The panel's hardest finding: these were silently _made_ by the draft and are actually Maurice's to make. **#1, #2, and #5 are now RESOLVED (2026-06-26); #3, #4, #6 remain.** No palette retint substitutes for them.

1. **Find vs make — RESOLVED: a sort/stamp/dispatch house, NOT a fabrication plant.** A full-screen industrial _plant_ (mastering racks, a "Voice Synthesis" machine, a forge) reads as _manufacturing bangers_ — the one thing the persona must never imply (VOICE: _"Fluncle finds it… the music isn't his… a finding is not itself the music"_). **Locked framing:** the works **examine, log, stamp, and launch** findings that **enter the belt from outside the building** (already a banger, arriving from Earth) — the customs-house / mail-sorting / observatory register; **nothing on screen _originates_ a banger.** Drop the literal "Voice Synthesis" machine (the recovered-audio observation is _Fluncle speaking_, not a machine making a voice) — render that station as the recovered booth/observatory, not a synth. Every machine label + the masthead + the tagline lean _examine/stamp/log/dispatch_, never _cast/forge/master/synthesize_. The **name follows** (§2a): "Foundry" is now disfavoured (a foundry forges = makes) — pick from the dispatch/observatory register.
2. **Scroll vs fixed painting — RESOLVED: a scrollable left-to-right belt.** The reference image is a fixed composition only so the whole works fit in one capture; the surface itself is the **scrollable belt** the brief, the ROADMAP ("a full-screen, **left-to-right conveyor**"), and `docs/factory.md` ("a 1-D pan camera… watched and scrolled") all specify. **This keeps v1's pan camera** and removes the largest cost driver the draft feared (the fixed-painting belt-polyline + per-deck anchors + arc-length LUT + portrait compromise are NOT needed) — Gate B becomes "richer scrolling belt," not "fixed multi-deck painting." `layout.ts` stays a 1-D `stationX(i)` line (generalize only for spacing/feel, not a polyline); `sim.ts`'s scalar `targetX` is unchanged. The **viewport** is the full-screen 1920×1080 stage (#5), and the belt world (`WORLD_W`) is **wider than 1920** so the camera pans across it.
3. **The hard machine roster.** The honesty rule ("no machine for a non-existent datum") + the real pipeline yield **~8 stages**, essentially v1's existing set: `intake · spectrograph · press · booth · render · dispatch · address · launch`. The reference's extras (Metadata Stamper, Quality Control, Packaging, Audio Mastering) map to **no datum** — they are exactly where the reference drifts into fiction. **Recommendation:** render the ~8 real stages as detailed, characterful, well-spaced machines, and fill the room with richly-textured _set-dressing_ (brick, windows, pipes, lamps, a stamp the cover passes under as flavour) — not more stations. Resolve this to an enumerated list before handoff; it sets the art count, the anchors, the aspect ratio, and the screen budget.
4. **The status panel / aggregate.** Recommendation: cut the panel (decision #2 of the reframe). If any whole-table fact survives (archive age, "N in the log"), it's one quiet plate or `sr-only`, fed by a minimal aggregate — not a metrics HUD.
5. **Render resolution — RESOLVED: a true full-screen 1920×1080 stage.** NOT v1's 320×180 chunky-upscaled logical canvas. The scene renders at a **1920×1080 (16:9) design stage** and the AI art is authored **detailed at native/high res** (16-bit-style pixel art, Nano Banana Pro 4K downscaled — the reference's fidelity, not Game-Boy chunk). On a 1920×1080 screen it's 1:1; other viewports **scale-to-fit (contain) and extend the room/wall/floor plates into the margins** so it reads edge-to-edge full-screen with no dead bars (the belt continues off-screen — it's a scroll). High-detail source means a smooth contain-downscale is fine; the old integer-snap rule only mattered for upscaling a tiny canvas, which we no longer do. **Engineering consequence:** v1's `VIEW_W/VIEW_H` (320×180) and its `image-rendering: pixelated` integer fit are replaced; the moving canvas now backs ~1920×1080, so the grain pass renders at reduced res and upscales, and the mid-phone perf trace is mandatory (see §3.E).
6. **Art model + budget + taste-loop owner.** Nano Banana Pro (4K, multi-reference) for the detailed plates vs flash-image for sprites; who runs the loop; roughly how many iterations are acceptable.

### 3.C — The AI-art pipeline

Fork `packages/skills/fluncle-sprites/scripts/generate_sprite.py` (the only generator that exists today) into a new `generate_scene.py` with **two asset classes sharing one palette**: `machine`/`prop`/`mascot` (reuse magenta-key→crop→resize→quantize→outline, but **bump to 48–96px and quantize to the whole `factoryPalette` (~16–18 swatches), not a 6-colour subset** — the tiny subset flattens detail to mush) and `plate`/`tile` (**new path: no key, no crop, no outline**; opaque, resize to an exact tile dimension, quantize to the same `factoryPalette`, **validate the seam** via an offset-wrap check — tiling must be prompted explicitly). **Anti-drift (the "one family" problem):** one shared palette post-quantized identically (the strongest lock); a **reference-image anchor** — generate every asset off one approved hero, never chain asset N off N-1 (errors compound); restate the full rails each prompt; small batches (3–6). Use **Nano Banana Pro / Gemini 3 Pro Image** (4K + multi-reference) for plates, flash-image for sprites; spike whether the request shape exposes a **seed**. Drop-in: the `factory` collection auto-mirrors; the Gate-0 loader makes every asset hot-swappable with zero further code change. **Pilot one machine (Gate 0) before fan-out.**

### 3.D — Canon / palette / voice (the retint spec)

Every surface samples the canon ramp; no new hues. Brick = warm-dark masonry (`creamDim #6e6657`/`goldDeep #7a5c00` faces, `tapeBlack`/`sleeveBlack` mortar) — **a hard rule: brick heat never touches `red #ff6b57`/`redBright` (Re-entry Red is errors-only); it lives at `redDeep #7a2418` and reads through _value_, not saturation.** Steel = cream-ramp (`creamMuted`/`creamDim`, `tapeBlack`/`deepField` seams, `#fffbf2`+`#090a0b` rivets) — **proven on the Gate-0 pilot** (the "steel vs clay" gate; value-contrast + heavy grain do the "metal," not a cool highlight). The one sun = `gold #f5b800`/`goldBright #ffd057` worklight tracking the active cover; windows = night cosmos (`makeVoidTile`) + the horizon eclipse (`gold→reentry-red`), the same single sun. Grain dense under content, near-silent over readout text (Legible Sky). **AA:** read off cream (`#f4ead7` on `#171611` ≈ 12:1), never off teal; verify against the _scanlined_ ground. **Type:** masthead nameplate in Oxanium 800 caps (the one sanctioned large heading, brand-mark caps OK); CRT readouts in Monaspace Krypton mono (the machine's voice); columnar numerals Oxanium tabular-nums. **Baked-vs-live:** anything you must _read_ is live DOM at the real font; flavour glyphs can be baked pixels.

**Voice — the label system (validated by the panel, two fixes):** machine status `[ ok ] line running` (lowercase deadpan, the `TerminalBody` register); the gold rotated `FLUNCLE CERTIFIED BANGER` stamp (brand-mark caps legal) / running "certified banger."; `EST. '90` (not 2023 — the persona's been at it since '90); mascot said-not-written ("more in the pipe. always is, fam."). **Fix 1:** never use "sector" as a UI label or structural noun (VOICE: it's first-person prose colour only) — drop earthly origin facts on labels instead. **Fix 2:** the tagline's "cast and stamped" leans _make_ — soften to the sort/dispatch register per decision #1. No banned words, no exclamation, no em dash, said-not-written hold.

### 3.E — Accessibility & performance

**A11y (independent of the render path):** expose a parallel `sr-only` live list of findings + their station names (so the pipeline state is navigable without parsing the canvas); extend `<noscript>` to render the SSR finding list (titles + `stationOf` station) as the real backlog in text. If the `-camX` DOM readout overlay ships, CRT text is real selectable AA text (the accessibility win); if readouts stay canvas-drawn, the `sr-only` list carries the same truth. Reduced-motion freezes ambient (slats, grain boil, blink, launch shake) and keeps truth (positions, the one active tell). **Performance (the full-screen 1920×1080 stage makes this real):** the moving canvas now backs ~1080p (≈16× v1's 320×180 pixel area), so the per-frame `grain.draw` is the cost to watch — **render grain at reduced res (e.g. quarter) and upscale it**, don't full-frame-noise 1080p every tick; cap the canvas backing store / DPR on mobile.
