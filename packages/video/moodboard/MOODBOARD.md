# Moodboard

Visual references for the Fluncle video direction, annotated so they read as a spec rather than vibes. The caption is the load-bearing part: every image names which texture family it feeds, what to steal, and what to ignore. The video agent reads this folder as canon alongside DESIGN.md; humans drop images in and caption them here.

## One galaxy, many regions

Every video is a log from a different reach of the traveller's run through time, space, and other dimensions — so no two should look alike. This board is NOT a single house style to average toward; it is a map of distinct REGIONS, each its own look that still reads as the same logbook. The signature is the canon, never the surface: warm dark ground, one light source (Eclipse Gold), Re-entry Red as heat, Starlight Cream as ink, grain on every frame (the Light-Years Rule), and exactly one travelling vehicle per video. Inside those constants, reach for a DIFFERENT region each time — a dusk horizon, a decaying sleeve, a dye-bleed portal, a velocity smear — rather than the blend of all of them. The texture families below are those regions; never collapse them into one mean.

## The Retint Rule

**The rule lives in [DESIGN.md](../../../DESIGN.md) (section 2, Named Rules) — that is the source of truth.** In short: steal the technique, recolor it to canon. Read it there; do not re-litigate it here.

How it applies to this board: almost every reference below carries off-canon cool hues (phosphor green, RGB primaries, broadcast blue, candy pink, emerald). Each entry's caption names the technique to steal — halftone, dot-matrix, scanlines, line displacement, liquid gradients, gel splits, mirror tiling — and the cool palette to ignore. Whatever you sample arrives in its own colors and leaves in ours: warm dark ground, Eclipse Gold as the single light source, Re-entry Red as the heat accent, Starlight Cream as the ink. Cool hues survive only as minor counter-accents.

## Texture families

- **nebula**: grainy gradient planets, space fields, rim-lit orbs.
- **analog**: VHS/CRT warmth, scanlines, film grain, broadcast decay.
- **dither**: halftone, bitmap, dot-matrix, pixel masking; the matrix pole.
- **paint**: hyper-saturated nostalgic illustration; the past pole, kept deliberately bright and always heavily caveated.
- **fluent**: flowing liquid gradients and forms; motion lives in the surface itself.
- **duotone**: strict two-color treatments, gel-split lighting.
- **smear**: velocity written into the frame — long-exposure / intentional-camera-movement blur; the journey rendered as motion itself, not as grain or line displacement.

## Framing devices

Several references share a way of HOLDING the finding that cuts across families — name them so a video can borrow one deliberately, independent of which texture region it lives in:

- **portal / threshold**: a framed window onto the warm side standing inside the dark — a mirror propped in foliage (`garden-mirror-sky-portal.png`), a receding doorway (`contour-threshold-doorway.png`), a dye-bleed aperture (`watercolor-dye-bleed-portal.png`), the first-party `posted/the-portal.jpg`. The Eclipse as a picture in a frame, brightening on the beat.
- **floating plate**: the finding held as a discrete object in the void, often with a soft ground reflection — `floating-emberlit-cloud-plate.png`. The log as a thing you could pick up and turn over.
- **sleeve-as-logbook**: a recovered record sleeve, its print marginalia (side, title, runtime, catalog stamp) surviving as the frame's archive typography while the image around it decays — `datamosh-sleeve-preservation.png`, `sleeve-margin-oleander-lightleak.png`, `scanline-heat-sleeve-diamond.png`. The found object IS the HUD.
