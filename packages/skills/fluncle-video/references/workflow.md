# Workflow: one track â†’ one video

The per-track runbook, end to end. You are given a trackId; you produce one 9:16 rendered MP4 with its own visual character, plus a source-backed output bundle that can be uploaded to R2 and rerendered later. You work inside a checkout of the Fluncle repo. Read [SKILL.md](../SKILL.md) (the doctrine) first; this is the procedure that applies it.

## 1. Props (the ground truth)

Run `bun run social:preview <trackId> --skip-render` from the repo root first. This resolves preview audio, analyzes it (BPM, beat grid, onsets, energy and bass curves), extracts the artwork palette, and writes `packages/video/out/<trackId>.props.json`.

Read that file. It is the ground truth for the music's energy and the track's colors â€” title, artists, `discoveredAt`, the swatches, and the `audio.*` arrays your hooks will read. If preview resolution fails, stop and report; there is no video without legal audio.

The default clip is 20s, and the default is a FALLBACK, not a decision â€” every recent cut has shipped at exactly 20.0s, which is the tell that the waveform was never consulted. **The cut is a per-track musical decision** (doctrine 8): read THIS track's energy curve, choose `--duration-ms <10000-30000>` for its shape, end on a drop or just before a transition, never mid-build â€” and name the duration decision in your report (even "20s, because the drop resolves at 19.2s"; a silent 20.0s is a miss). The analyzer now ships the drop for you: `audio.dropMs` (a breakdownâ†’slam novelty detection) plus `audio.dropCandidates` (all scored, for an alternate climax) and `audio.downbeats` (bar-level pulses) â€” those are the moments your vehicle ignites, and the drop envelope defaults to `audio.dropMs`, so you only hand-pick a peak when overriding.

## 2. Metadata (Spotify, via the props)

No web search. The facts come from the track's Spotify metadata, surfaced in the props file, and **the metadata must visibly matter** (doctrine 5). You are mining two kinds of material:

- **FACTS** that may appear on screen: release year and label, when present in the props. They are authoritative â€” straight from Spotify, the source of truth â€” so they need no citation and carry no alias-collision risk. Render only fields the props actually expose; never invent one. The track metadata (title, artists, album, Found date) is always safe.
- **CREATIVE FUEL** that never renders as text: the artwork palette the pipeline extracts, the album and label names, the track title, and the shape of the energy curve. This shapes your vehicle, texture family, palette lean, and scene concept. Your run report must **name how it drove specific visual decisions** so the operator can see it in the pixels.

BPM comes from the audio analysis already in the props â€” measured off the actual clip, beat-grid aligned, more accurate than any metadata tempo; that is what your vehicle syncs to. Musical **Key**, if the props expose it, is an optional DJ touch on the overlay â€” never required, silently omitted when absent.

## 3. Concept (vehicle FIRST)

In order:

1. **Diversity check (doctrine 3) â€” anchor on the feed neighbour, judge the picture.** Run `fluncle admin tracks vehicles --json` â€” the recently-used findings, newest-found first, i.e. in feed order (videos post in found-date / Log ID order, so the top entry is the thumbnail that sits RIGHT NEXT TO yours). Each entry now carries `register` beside `vehicle` and `grain` (the three diversity axes; older entries have `register` absent). FETCH AND VIEW the posters of that immediate predecessor and the last ~3 (`curl -s "https://found.fluncle.com/<log-id>/poster.jpg" -o /tmp/ref-<log-id>.jpg`, then view) â€” compare the THUMBNAILS, not the vehicle words, because two different labels routinely render as the same soft smear. The immediate neighbour is the HARD constraint: choose a vehicle clearly distinct from it in **palette AND form AND contrast** (a rhyme 4â€“5 findings back is acceptable; the immediate neighbour is not). **Swing the structural register, don't repaint it:** soft, blurry, low-contrast liquid (fog / smoke / membrane folds) is the basin these renders collapse into run after run â€” if the last one or two were that, make THIS one legible hard structure WITH DEPTH and higher contrast (dot-screen, dither/halftone, crisp-walled cellular, lines-with-depth); recolouring the same fog is not a new vehicle. Sequential runs converge on the soft-liquid attractor like parallel runs converge on voronoi/horizontal-lines unless each deliberately diverges. Read the REGISTER across those posters too â€” fully-abstract / representational / framing-device â€” the third diversity axis beside vehicle and grain (doctrine 3): the fleet has been living 100% fully-abstract, so if the last two findings were fully-abstract fields, reach for the representational register or a framing device (a portal, the sleeve-as-logbook, a floating plate, a horizon â€” cookbook Â§framing devices). Also honor the brief and the SKILL.md failure modes.
2. **Commit to the whole-frame register, THEN the driver (doctrine 1).** First decide what the WHOLE frame is: **fully abstract** (the field itself is the image, edge to edge) or **recognizably real, shown abstractly** (the eye can name it â€” a flock, flowers, a wing); never the uncanny middle, and never a small object on a dead background. Then pick the ONE driver inside it â€” the full-field surface ITSELF, or a focal subject that integrates the whole frame. orb / lines / fractal / glass / glitch, or one you invent; everything else supports it. Let `track.features` (the enrichment spectral summary in props, when present) steer the choice â€” a sub-bass-heavy track (`subBassRatio`) wants a dense, heavy vehicle; a bright high-`centroidHz` one wants treble sparkle and fine detail; a busy high-`onsetRate` one wants a snappier field. Decide how the one committed climax is expressed â€” the vehicle's OWN material intensifying in the SCENE's palette (a crest igniting, a resolution front completing, a body's limb surging), never a second celestial body and never a bolted-on gold/yellow sun. Eclipse Gold is optional, reached only when the vehicle's own material genuinely runs hot; a cool scene climaxes cool. **Then name the DEPTH MECHANISM and the LANDING POINT** (the whole-frame law): what makes this field a SPACE rather than a surface â€” a lighting direction, volumetric falloff, parallax/scale layers, a raymarched body, atmospheric occlusion â€” and where the eye LANDS, the one privileged region (denser, brighter, deeper than the rest), which is also where the climax lives. A concept that can name neither is wallpaper before it renders (the corner-swap test, SKILL.md failure modes). A framing device (portal, sleeve, plate, horizon) can hold either register â€” and it is the natural register-rotation move after a run of fully-abstract fields.
3. **Choose the texture family** (nebula / analog / dither / paint / fluent / duotone / smear â€” see MOODBOARD.md) that fits the tune. A liquid roller wants drift, nebula, or fluent; a neuro stomper wants dither, glitch, hard onset flashes; a gel-lit duotone suits the meditative ones; a headlong, propulsive track suits smear (velocity blur). Pick a DIFFERENT region than the last few renders â€” the moodboard is a map of distinct looks, not an average to converge on.
4. **Write a two-sentence journey:** from where, through what, arriving where â€” matched to the song's energy curve. Name where the drop lands and how the vehicle ignites there.
5. **Read the music's arc (doctrine 10) â€” drive the arc from the live envelopes.** The energy curve and the drop are the clip's shape. Drive the vehicle's structural arc â€” density, threshold, width, ignition, exposure, framing â€” from the live audio uniforms (`u_energy` / `u_audioSwell` / `u_audioDrop`) so the build IS the music, the same on any track. Place the drop crest with the `reactivity.drop` envelope (`{ peakTimeMs, riseMs, holdMs, fallMs }`), which derives the smoothed `u_audioDrop` â€” the sanctioned home for the crest timestamp. The envelope's peak DEFAULTS to `audio.dropMs` (the analyzer's detected drop), so leave `peakTimeMs` unset unless you're deliberately overriding to a different `audio.dropCandidates` moment. Keep hardcoded `sec`-timeline structure (the "scripted-clock arc") for the journey baseline only; a vehicle whose density or ignition is a JS `interpolate(sec, â€¦)` aligns with the music only on the one track it was tuned to. List the key timestamps in your header comment as a read, not as constants the structure indexes.
6. **Write the reactivity map (doctrine 9).** Use `useAudioReactivity(audio, profile)` unless there is a specific reason not to. Name at least one structural reaction (`u_audioHit`/`u_audioSwell` changes width, density, radius, scale, threshold), one light reaction (glow, exposure, climax intensity), and one texture reaction (grain, dither, chroma, edge roughness). Split the THREE frequency bands across elements (`u_bass`/`useBass` kick â†’ one element, `u_mid`/`useMid` lead â†’ another, `u_treble`/`useTreble` hats â†’ fine detail/sparkle) so the clip PLAYS the music, not just moves with it. Audio disturbs the material IN PLACE â€” that is where ALL the reactivity belongs; GLOBAL translation (travel, flow, convergence) stays an audio-free constant clock by default (doctrine 7). If audio MUST bend translation, only a smoothed envelope with a SMALL coefficient over a dominant constant base, never the raw per-beat transient.
7. **Write the render intent (`out/<trackId>.intent.json`).** Capture the reactivity map as a machine-readable record at `out/<trackId>.intent.json` (schema `fluncle.render-intent/1`): the `vehicle`, `textureFamily`, `register`, `arcSource: "energyCurve"`, `motionModel`, `dropMs` (default to the analyzer's `audio.dropMs`), the `climax` {form, colour drawn FROM the scene, atMs}, and the `bindings[]` â€” one entry per `{band, element, axis, intendedStrength}`. Cover â‰Ą1 structural axis (width/threshold/warpAmp/wallSharpness/density/radius/scale/curvature), â‰Ą1 light axis (brightness/exposure/glow/ignition), and â‰Ą1 texture axis (grain/chroma/dither/edgeRough). Any binding with `axis: "translation"` MUST use a smoothed band (`swell`/`drop`/`energy`). This file is the contract the render is checked against and is shipped into the bundle (`ship` copies `out/<trackId>.intent.json` â†’ `intent.json`; a missing intent is warn-and-stub in v1, not a blocker). **Lint it before you render:** `bun run --cwd packages/video validate:intent out/<trackId>.intent.json` is the strict per-field check (schema, the `textureFamily`/`register`/`arcSource`/`motionModel` enums, each `bindings[]` entry's `band`/`axis`/`intendedStrength`) â€” exit `0` valid, `1` invalid, `2` usage error. The frozen schema lives in `packages/video/src/pipeline/intent.ts` (`type RenderIntent`); the shader's header comment may mirror it for human read.

Confirm the **Always-Visible Vehicle** (doctrine 2) in the concept: the vehicle fills the frame from the first frame (a dim ember, a flat field, a scrambled matrix that is unmistakably present), never a late reveal.

## 4. Author the composition (in the gitignored workbench)

Create your composition at `packages/video/src/remotion/workbench/<CompId>.tsx`. `workbench/` is gitignored and `root.tsx` AUTO-REGISTERS every `.tsx` in it â€” **the composition id is the filename** (so `workbench/caustic-weave.tsx` renders as `--composition caustic-weave`). You never touch `root.tsx`, and because workbench is gitignored there is nothing to clean up and no commit hazard. `ship` copies the exact source into `packages/video/out/<log-id>/composition.tsx`; R2 keeps the durable copy.

- Pick a unique **kebab-case** `<CompId>` for the filename (e.g. `caustic-weave.tsx` â†’ `--composition caustic-weave`) â€” the repo's `oxlint` `unicorn/filename-case` gate REJECTS PascalCase filenames, and the composition id is simply whatever the filename is. **`export default`** your `React.FC<NostalgicCosmosProps>` (reuse the contract unchanged so the pipeline feeds it).
- Import ONLY from `../cosmos` (plus `remotion`, `react`, `@fluncle/tokens`). No styled vehicles, no static image assets.
- Write your scene and your GLSL shader. Lean on [cookbook.md](cookbook.md) for technique and the `GLSL.*` inventory.
- Honor the quad law (doctrine 6): every `ShaderLayer` drives color AND alpha to 0.0 inside its quad. Motion law (doctrine 7) for any background/field you author yourself (a star drift, motes â€” the core ships no Starfield): a smooth baseline drift that audio may bend only through smoothed envelopes, never a raw-beat snap.
- Render the facts through `TypePlate` (doctrine 4): one drop-in, fixed homes, prescriptive timing â€” pass scene-derived `ink`/`dimInk`, and nudge `identityInSec`/`telemetryInSec` onto a musical seam if the intro asks. Never hand-place the facts with raw `FloatingType`.
- End with `CloseCard`, driven by the journey's `"arrive"` phase â€” it owns its lower-left home; pass it the scene `palette` for ink + emphasis accent.
- **Play the audio (MANDATORY):** drop `<TrackAudio audio={audio} />` in once. The hooks drive only the VISUALS; this is what makes the render carry SOUND. Omit it and the cut is silent (the render fails on a silence check). `ship` keeps audio in both masters; TikTok's silent cut is derived on the fly by an `audio=false` Media Transformation, not stored.
- **Determinism:** remotion `random(seed)` and frame-derived values only. Never `Math.random` / `Date.now` / `new Date()`. Audio reactivity only through the hooks.
- **Reactivity bus:** prefer `useAudioReactivity` and pass its `uniforms` into `ShaderLayer` (or pass `onsets`/`reactivity` directly to `ShaderLayer` for built-in `u_audio*` uniforms). Map those uniforms to material disturbance â€” width, density, threshold, radius, warp amplitude, wall sharpness, refraction, grain, dither, glow â€” and route the fast bands (`u_bassFast`/`u_midFast`/`u_trebleFast`, `u_audioHit`, `u_onsetPulse`) onto in-place STRUCTURAL deformation as sharply as the music hits (the gate-safe binding idiom). Keep GLOBAL translation (travel, flow, convergence) an audio-free constant clock â€” `u_time` / `arc` / a steady eased rise â€” by default; if audio must bend it at all, only the smoothed envelopes (`u_audioSwell` / `u_audioDrop` / `u_energy`) with a small coefficient over a dominant constant base, never the raw per-beat transient (doctrine 7).
- **The live-ready authoring rule (for scenes meant to replay live).** The archive's clips are logged findings; a live set is Fluncle _dreaming_ â€” the same worlds re-driven behind the decks. For a body to replay live (Unit S, `scene.json` `liveReady: true`), it MUST read **HEADER UNIFORMS ONLY** â€” every `u_*` it samples is one the header injects (`u_time`, `u_progress`, `u_energy`, the bands, `u_audio*`, `u_seed`, `u_palette`, â€¦). This is the scripted-clock law (doctrine 7) made mechanical: a custom `uniform float u_rise;`/`u_settle;` driven by clip-time JS has no live driver, so the emitter marks the scene not-live-ready. Where you would reach for a JS `interpolate(sec, â€¦)` on a custom uniform, express the arc on `u_progress` (offline: clip progress; live: the track playhead â€” the same uniform, both hosts) or the live envelopes instead. Not-live-ready still ships (the manifest carries the body + replay metadata); it just can't be re-driven live.
- Open the file with a header comment that states, at minimum: the track and label, the **vehicle** (so the next agent's diversity check works), the texture family, and the two-sentence concept. Keep it useful as future rerender context because this exact file is shipped as `composition.tsx`.

No registration step â€” dropping the file in `workbench/` is the registration (root.tsx globs it). Nothing to add to `root.tsx`, nothing to remove afterward.

## 5. Still-critique loop (minimum two rounds)

Render stills across the timeline and **view them** â€” code review alone misses overflow and blowouts:

```
bunx remotion still src/remotion/index.ts <CompId> out/still-N.png --props=out/<trackId>.props.json --frame=N
```

GPU shaders need a GL context â€” ANGLE locally / swangle on a GPU-less host, inherited from `remotion.config.ts` via `FLUNCLE_GL` (no `--gl` flag needed). Render at least four frames across the clip, and ALWAYS include:

- **frame ~5** â€” verify the vehicle is present and holding center (Always-Visible Vehicle).
- **the brightest frame** (the drop, from the energy curve) â€” verify type legibility and that nothing is blown out, that the warm dark holds. Verify the vehicle's FOCAL MASS sits inside the frame â€” off-centre is good, but the centre of attention must not be cropped out of frame (a crescent whose body lives past the edge reads as a missed framing). Check the corners: the full-bleed field must reach all four â€” if the frame reads as a circle/porthole, the background vignette is too tight (doctrine 6).
- **stills across the arc** (doctrine 10) â€” one at the open, one mid-build, one at the crest; side by side they should read as the SAME world evolving (the energy curve's rise carried into density, palette lean, scale, exposure), not a static loop. If the three are INTERCHANGEABLE, the clip is dead, not calm â€” a 20s near-static field has shipped exactly this way; the arc must be visible in the stills, with a climax the crest still can point to. Composition check (doctrine 11): if the vehicle is symmetric/radial, the symmetry holds the whole clip (centre doesn't wander off-axis); if it has a single focal mass, that mass is dead-centre or decisively off-centre, never _slightly_ off. (A translational/continuous vehicle is exempt from both.)
- **a mid-travel frame** â€” check the vehicle reads as STRUCTURE WITH DEPTH â€” neither a flat diagram of thin hard primitives nor a formless warm fog (SKILL.md failure-modes) â€” and run the **corner-swap test**: if the frame's four corners could be swapped without anyone noticing, the field is uniform wallpaper â€” give it its depth mechanism and landing point before another tuning round. Check the type ink is drawn from the composition's palette and there is NO gold type anywhere. Across your frames, confirm the `TypePlate` blocks sit in their fixed homes (identity lower-left, telemetry upper-right with the Log ID), both legible over the scene and both gone before the drop â€” and verify the TELEMETRY block's contrast SPECIFICALLY, not just the identity block: telemetry has shipped near-invisible; if the Found date + Log ID don't read at a glance, raise that ink. Check the clip evolves continuously with the energy curve, never holding a static loop (doctrine 10).
- **the ground colour and the grain, on PIXELS** â€” sample the rendered still's darks (an eyedropper, or average a dark crop): the ground must be a warm near-black (the Warm Dark Rule). A cool / blue-leaning field fails canon â€” cool survives only as a minor counter-accent, never the ground â€” and cool-dominant fields (navy bars, blue-lavender nets, teal webs) have been slipping through code review for exactly this reason: the palette read warm in the code and rendered cool. Judge the pixels, not the hex constants. Then shrink the same still to THUMBNAIL size (the actual feed surface) and confirm the grain is visibly there â€” a vector-clean thumbnail fails the Light-Years Rule (the perceptible floor, doctrine 3).

Iterate until type is legible inside the safe inset (the telemetry block included), the palette stays warm and inky on the pixels, grain is present at thumbnail scale, and the vehicle reads from frame one. Two critique rounds minimum; taste is part of the job.

**Aim, don't discover â€” and pivot the primitive, don't tweak a fragile one.** Decide the **brightness + density intent across the arc up front** (what the open vs the breakdown vs the drop should each look like) before tuning, so you're steering toward a target, not stumbling onto one. Then the convergence rule: **if 2â€“3 tuning rounds don't converge, the vehicle/primitive is wrong â€” pivot it, don't keep tweaking constants.** Oscillating between two bad poles (a smooth bright blob â†” dark and flat) is the tell that the underlying primitive can't hit the target (dots are a classic offender â€” see the dots-as-overlay note in the cookbook); change the primitive (a continuous ridged-filament or cellular field instead) rather than grinding the same one for ten rounds.

**The iteration ladder: stills â†’ draft â†’ one full ship render.** Stills (seconds) settle the static look â€” composition, palette, framing across the arc. They CANNOT show motion, reactivity, or whether the drop lands. Don't reach for the slow ship render to check those: render a **draft** (`bun run social:preview <trackId> --composition <CompId> --draft` â†’ `out/<trackId>.draft.mp4`, half-res, ~6Ă— faster, full motion + real audio) and eyeball motion + run the beat-pull gate on it (`detect-beat-pull out/<trackId>.draft.mp4`) for a FAST directional read while you iterate on the fix. Caveat: the draft is half-res, so it can UNDER-REPORT reversal on fine-stipple / live-grain clips (the churn the gate scores only fully manifests at full res â€” measured: a clip read 0.15 on the draft but 0.23 on the full). So the draft gate is DIRECTIONAL, not authoritative â€” the PASS verdict is always the full render's `judge:metrics` gate (step 7). Only once stills + the draft look right do you spend the one full ship render â€” which confirms the gate AND is the only thing that can judge the load-bearing grain. Drafts never ship (half-res/jpeg hide the grain; `ship` refuses a draft-only).

**Stills cannot show motion jitter or reactivity.** After the gates, scrub the MP4 (or render 3â€“4 ADJACENT frames around a beat) to confirm the vehicle FLOWS â€” position advances smoothly, audio only brightens/widens, nothing jumps-and-snaps on the kick (Motion law, doctrine 7). Then the **reactivity gate** (doctrine 9): play the MP4 against the audio â€” first confirm it HAS audible audio at all (the render's silence check guards this, but listen), then ask: can you FEEL the kick and the drop in the picture? If the motion would look the same with the sound muted, the reactivity is too weak; push it. But the motion itself must stay SMOOTH â€” if the picture jitters/lurches on the beat, audio is wrongly driving speed or position; move that reactivity into brightness/size/thickness/curvature instead (doctrine 7). Catch this here by eye, but the **metrics gate at step 7** (`judge:metrics`, which folds in beat-pull) is the objective backstop â€” a clip that lurches will fail it. Also confirm the clip's evolution EASES with the energy curve â€” a continuous build, never a static hold or a hard step (doctrine 10). And the climax check (doctrine 1): the bright moment must read as the VEHICLE igniting (its own material intensifying in the scene's palette), not a round sun-glow layered on top â€” and if the scene is cool, the climax stays cool, with no gold/yellow reached for. Deleting the vehicle should delete the bright moment. Finally the **swap test**: compare against recent run reports, R2 composition artifacts, or operator-provided stills when available. If the climax blooms in the same place, or the type sits in the same spot, or the geometry rhymes â€” change it.

## 6. Gates

Three pre-render gates, all blocking:

- `bun run --cwd packages/video typecheck`
- `bunx oxlint packages/video`
- `bun run --cwd packages/video lint:composition src/remotion/workbench/<CompId>.tsx` â€” the static motion lint. It scans your composition source (JS + GLSL) for any global translation term that binds audio over (or without) a dominant constant clock â€” the two whole-vehicle JUMP bugs of doctrine 7 â€” and exits non-zero on a finding. Fix the binding (move the reactivity into in-place material; keep global drift an audio-free constant clock), don't argue with it: it catches at author time what `judge:metrics` would fail after a full render.

Format with `bunx oxfmt --write <files>`.

## 7. Render

`bun run social:preview <trackId> --composition <CompId>` and **wait for the encode to finish** â€” renders take minutes and the MP4 is invalid until the process exits. (`<CompId>` is your `workbench/<CompId>.tsx` filename; the source is auto-resolved from there, so `--composition-source` is no longer needed.) The render writes `out/<trackId>.mp4` and `out/<trackId>.render.json`. Confirm with `ffprobe`: 1080Ă—1920, h264, aac audio, 15â€“30s.

**The metrics gate (MANDATORY â€” do not skip, do not ship past a fail).** Your eyes at step 5 are the first line, but three defects are temporal artifacts a scrub can miss â€” beat-pull, a photosensitivity-unsafe strobe, and a dead/frozen arc â€” so all three are gated objectively in ONE run:

```
bun run --cwd packages/video judge:metrics <trackId>
```

It writes `out/<trackId>.metrics.json` and carries THREE hard gates; any one exits non-zero and BLOCKS ship:

- **Beat-pull** (Motion law, doctrine 7): the clip's short-lag motion reversal â€” whether the picture jumps then snaps back, jittering back and forth (the lurch-and-snap). It does NOT punish a crisp surge that hits on the beat and flows on (that's doctrine 9, and it's wanted); only motion that UNDOES itself fails. A **fail** means a per-beat signal is driving position/travel/flow: find it (a raw `beat`/`hit`/`onset`/`u_audioBeat`/`u_audioHit` or a raised `swellBeatWeight` feeding a transform), move it into material (brightness/width/scale/glow â€” doctrine 7), and re-render.
- **Flash safety** (WCAG 2.3.1 / ISO 9241-391): a coherent, large-area, >3/sec luminance or saturated-red strobe â€” measured on a sliding 10Â° sub-window, so a flashing corner can't slip it. This is the photosensitivity gate: non-negotiable, and it is what stands between an over-driven bind and shipping a seizure risk. A **fail** means the reactivity is strobing whole regions â€” smooth the offending bind (the beat-jitter failure mode) and re-render. NEVER pass `--allow-flash` on a ship candidate.
- **Arc / deadness** (the structural TWIN of beat-pull, on the LONG timescale): it samples anchor frames across the clip and fails when the picture never structurally REORGANIZES â€” a frozen field, a looping wallpaper, near-static bars (`wholeClipChange < ARC_FLOOR`). Beat-pull kills a clip that lurches; this kills a clip that never moves. A **fail** means the vehicle isn't evolving with the music â€” drive the structural arc off the live envelopes (doctrine 10) so the field visibly builds and crests, and re-render. This is the objective backstop for the "interchangeable stills" / uniform-wallpaper failure.

The report also carries the advisory reads â€” coupling (does the picture's structural change track the music, against a permutation null) and the intent check (the rendered pixels against `out/<trackId>.intent.json`). They don't block, but a `dead` coupling verdict on a clip that also feels dead is your cue to sharpen the material bind (doctrine 9) before spending the taste pass. `detect-beat-pull` remains available as the fast directional read on the half-res draft (step 5); the PASS that counts is `judge:metrics` on the full render. Iterate until it passes. (Inconclusive â€” too few frames â€” passes; that's fine.)

## 8. The taste pass (the doom-scroll check)

The gates prove the clip is LEGAL; this step asks whether it is ALIVE â€” the mild-render failure mode applied at ship time. Before ship, put the new frame in its real context:

1. Pull the 3 feed-neighbour posters (already fetched at step 3: `https://found.fluncle.com/<log-id>/poster.jpg`) and place your render's poster frame NEXT TO them at thumbnail size â€” a 4-up contact strip, or simply view them small, side by side, the way the feed will.
2. Answer the doom-scroll question honestly: **would a thumb stop on yours?** Not "is it compliant" â€” does it GRAB, next to what it will actually sit beside?
3. For an objective second opinion, run `bun run --cwd packages/video judge:diversity <posterPath>` â€” it scores your poster's structural distance from those same feed neighbours (edge-orientation-dominant, so a recolour of the same form scores LOW even with a new palette). It is ADVISORY (it does not block ship), but a low distance to the immediate neighbour confirms the "safe wallpaper" suspicion â€” swing the structural register, don't repaint it (doctrine 3).

If it reads as safe wallpaper next to them â€” same density everywhere, nothing to land on, a recolour of the neighbours' energy â€” go back and iterate, and bias toward the BOLDER move: a stronger landing point, a deeper depth mechanism, a harder material bind, a register swing. Rule-compliant â‰  good; this is the last moment to catch a forgettable clip before the feed does.

## 9. Ship (package, upload, link)

Once the render passes its gates and the taste pass, package the bundle and link it to the track. All local; the operator runs it.
