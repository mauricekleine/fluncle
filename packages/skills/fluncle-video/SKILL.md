# Fluncle Video

Use this skill to make Fluncle's per-track social videos: 1080×1920 vertical clips that put one banger under the burning eclipse, every one unmistakably Fluncle, no two looking alike. **This skill is the constitution.** The package (`packages/video`) is a near-blank canvas — pure machinery + the fixed info layer, no imposed look — and the agent writes the scene and the shader for each track itself. More creative freedom, more diversity; the doctrine lives here.

This file is a router. Read it top-down, then load the reference for the phase you are in. The doctrine below is authoritative; where a living doc in the repo adds detail, follow it, but the rulings here win on conflict.

## The soul — each video is a log entry

Before any craft, know what you are making. A Fluncle video is NOT a music visualizer or a motion-graphics loop — it is a **LOG ENTRY**. The traveller is far out, alone, keeping a diary of a run through deep space and other dimensions; each banger is a FINDING, and the clip is the page where ONE thing witnessed out there is recorded — a phenomenon **observed, logged, and felt**. The Log ID (lower-left, via `TypePlate`) is the entry's coordinate; the `CloseCard` signs it off; the grain is the proof it travelled to reach us. The register is **observational and emotional** — wonder, vast solitude, the sublime, a little melancholy — never hype, never a UI demo. So stage ONE phenomenon (the vehicle) as a thing genuinely SEEN through degraded glass at the edge of the map, and let the music be its OWN pulse: the picture RESPONDS to the sound the way a real thing breathes with a force passing through it — the whole field SWAYS and SURGES with the music, and its material FLINCHES sharply on the kick (a crack widening, a thread fattening, a node flaring) the way a real surface answers a force striking it. (This is the Motion law felt as soul: the picture's TRANSLATION glides; its material may answer the beat as sharply as the sound hits.) And when the page records a SUBJECT — a hull, a ruin, a distant creature — the split sharpens: the world answers the music while the thing keeps its own clock, because the music is the traveller's own pulse while watching, never the subject's choreography (the three-clocks law, doctrine 7). One phenomenon, observed, logged, alive. If the clip reads as a reactive-shader demo rather than a page torn from a logbook, it has missed the soul — fix that before anything else.

## Why this exists

A styled component library makes agents produce same-looking videos: identical text spots and timing, default eclipse discs, the same vehicle run after run. The best output comes from an agent writing its own shader from scratch. So there is no styled scene library — the package is the core surface, and the rules that components used to enforce are doctrine you uphold by hand. Diversity is the point. If your video could be swapped with another and nobody would notice, you have failed.

The aesthetic is already captured and it is strong — warm dark, grain, the through-the-glass calm. **Trust it, and use it freely.** The rulings below fence the brand; the space inside them is wide and it is yours. Lean toward the bolder, more alive idea over the safe repeat — a render that is unmistakably Fluncle yet unlike the recent output is the win. The doctrine is a short list of laws plus the failure modes that recur, not a recipe to follow step by step.

**The north star: FLUID, ORGANIC, ALIVE, and a little ALIEN.** This is the strongest pull of the whole brand — the footage should look like something Fluncle FOUND living on a far world, **recovered footage from the far edge of the map**, not something a machine drew. Favour flowing, breathing, almost-biological motion and form over the rigid, gridded, and mechanical. A structure is welcome (an iris, a weave, a dot screen) — but make it _breathe_: let lines curve and bow, let a lattice flex and ripple, let edges soften and reform. When a vehicle has a rigid state and a fluid state, LEAD with the fluid one. Caustics, smoke, liquid light, a curving membrane — these are the home register; treat hard geometry as something the music is melting, not holding still. One carve-out: when the subject IS architecture — a hull, a ruin, a monolith — the music melts the MEDIUM between you and it (the atmosphere, the glass, the grain), never the thing itself; its stillness against a living world is the alien.

**The whole frame IS the composition (not a vehicle on a background).** The 1080×1920 frame is ONE integrated image, edge to edge — and this is the law the weakest renders break, by dropping a small "vehicle" object onto an otherwise dead, static field. It reads as isolated and wrong. Commit the WHOLE frame to one of two registers and make the entire canvas that thing:

1. **Fully abstract** — the texture/field itself IS the image, filling the frame: a vein-screen, a caustic field, a drifting nebula, a stipple swarm that OWNS the canvas. No subject; the surface is the subject.
2. **Recognizably real, shown abstractly — nameable AND PLACED (redefined 2026-07-04).** It reads as a real, NAMEABLE thing seen through Fluncle's degraded glass — and the eye must be able to name it AND place it: the subject has a spatial relation to the observer — scale, distance, a viewpoint, occlusion, a ground or horizon or void it sits in. A texture that merely RESEMBLES a material (basalt cross-sections, pressure strata, an echogram raster) is NOT representational — it is abstract with a caption; declare it abstract. Presence-class subjects: a ruin on a plain, a vast indifferent hull crossing the field, tree-like growths against a glowing sky, a distant creature half-lost in fog, a threshold you are approaching (DESIGN.md's own recurring motifs — the lone figure against vastness, portal/threshold framing — live here). The check is the **stranger test**: a stranger glancing at the shipped poster says "there is a THING there," names it as a kind, and says where it sits relative to them (near, far, above, receding). The legibility target (operator ruling, 2026-07-04): **nameable as a KIND, alien in every detail** — "a ship," "trees," "ruins," never a specific earthly referent (the legibility ladder below).

The render-killer is the **uncanny middle**: a half-real approximation that convinces as neither — strange blobs-with-lines that look like nothing at all. So: if you reach for the representational register, it must GENUINELY resemble the thing (lean on real structure — a real flock's streaming rivers, a real petal's form, a real hull's mass); and when a subject starts sliding toward the middle, retreat along the presence-PRESERVING ladder, in order: (a) pull it to DISTANCE and SILHOUETTE — a matte dark mass against a live sky cannot be uncanny, because a silhouette demands no interior detail to get wrong; (b) bury it one layer deeper in the TREATMENT (fog, dither, bloom) until only the gesture survives; (c) only then drop to fully abstract. Silhouette-and-distance is almost always reachable before abstraction is. Everything below — the "one driver", its climax, its motion — operates INSIDE this law. "One vehicle" is a SUB-CASE of it (the focal-subject mode, where the subject still has to integrate the whole frame), never licence to float a lone object on a dead backdrop.

**Depth and a landing point (inside this law).** Owning the whole frame is not licence to be FLAT. A statistically uniform texture — same density, same brightness, every region interchangeable — is the sticker failure inverted: a field with no subject anywhere (the uniform-wallpaper failure below). Every concept MUST name its **depth mechanism** — a lighting direction, volumetric falloff, parallax/scale layers, a raymarched body, atmospheric occlusion — something that makes the field a SPACE, not a surface — and the **landing point** for the eye: the region the composition privileges, which is also where the climax lives. A field can own the whole frame AND still have somewhere to land; a field with nowhere to land is wallpaper, however alive its texture.

**The layer-split law (how treatment abstracts without killing the name — ratified 2026-07-04).** Every reference that reads as "a real thing seen through degraded glass" splits the image into two layers and abuses only one. The treatment attacks the SURFACE freely — texture (grain and dither eat it), hue (retint it anywhere the Retint Rule allows), resolution (bloom, blur, edge-width inflation), time (one-way smear, persistence echoes). It NEVER attacks the three carriers of the name: the **silhouette** (edges, branching structure — a dithered edge may dissolve as a density-fade OF the true silhouette, never a redraw of it), the **depth cues** (occlusion, ONE consistent light direction every gradient obeys, atmospheric perspective — losing a single cue can drop a form two rungs even under a perfect silhouette), and the **luma ordering** (every retint is a MONOTONIC luma→hue ramp — hue may go anywhere, brightness order never reorders; `GLSL.rampRetint` is this rule as code). Spend the whole abstraction budget on surface and the subject survives total surface loss — petals blown to white voids still read as flowers while their notched edges hold. Two working rules ride on the split. **The anchor rule:** legibility is frame-relative — ONE sharp, high-frequency element (a bristled stalk, an antenna, a rigging line, a foreground datum) licenses every other form in the frame to melt, because the eye attributes the softness to optics rather than vagueness; a frame of only-melted forms collapses to fog no matter how coherent each form is. **The kernel rule:** a surface treatment is safe while its kernel (blur radius, dither pitch, grain clump, smear length) stays under ~⅓ of the subject's smallest feature; at kernel ≈ feature the subject drops a rung; at kernel ≥ the subject's major axis the name dies.

**The legibility ladder** — the shared vocabulary for how much name survives:

| Rung | Name                  | What survives                                       | Viewer says               |
| ---- | --------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| 5    | Species-nameable      | silhouette + internal structure + plausible colour  | "a yellow daisy"          |
| 4    | Object-nameable       | silhouette + shading; surface destroyed             | "flowers", "an hourglass" |
| 3    | Category-nameable     | silhouette-by-occlusion + correlated structure only | "mountains", "a ship"     |
| 2    | Presence-without-name | coherent form + depth cues, no stable silhouette    | "something is there"      |
| 1    | Field                 | nothing correlated, nothing occluding               | "…colours"                |

The operator's target (2026-07-04): **distant presences at rung 2–3 behind a rung-4 anchor** — nameable as a KIND, alien in every detail. That combination is, mechanically, the "want to peek around the corner" feeling: certainty that something exists (occlusion + coherence) with the name withheld (silhouette at the kernel threshold), and an anchor proving the withholding is optics. Rung 1 everywhere is the uniform-wallpaper failure; rung 5 up close is the polished-CGI risk. The canonical treatment references are five moodboard images, each an obeyed proof of the split — `crt-cosmos-bloom-coral.png` (rung 4 held under total surface loss, one light every gradient obeys), `dither-hourglass-glitch.png` (a nameable relic with no contour line anywhere — the silhouette is a density-fade), `dreamy-blurred-wildflowers.png` (the whole ladder in one frame, plus the anchor rule), `grain-pink-carnival-mask-bloom.png` (legibility surviving the most violent retints because every ramp is monotonic), `waveform-ridge.png` (a landform conjured bottom-up from correlation + occlusion + an envelope — the existence proof that shaders with no photographs reach "nameable"). Study those five before authoring a presence render. Scope ruling (2026-07-04): the presence direction lands on the offline per-track videos first; live scenes inherit it later through the normal scene-manifest path — keep the live-ready authoring rule intact along the way.

## The two things that are always true

1. **The video IS the output bundle.** Author each video as one self-contained composition at `src/remotion/workbench/<CompId>.tsx` — `export default` the component, import only the core from `../cosmos`. `workbench/` is gitignored and `root.tsx` AUTO-REGISTERS everything in it (the composition id is the filename — use a **kebab-case** filename like `caustic-weave.tsx`, since `oxlint`'s `unicorn/filename-case` gate rejects PascalCase), so you NEVER edit `root.tsx`, a render touches no tracked file, and there is nothing to clean up. After render, `ship` copies that exact source to `packages/video/out/<log-id>/composition.tsx` with `props.json`, `render.json`, and the footage; the R2 bundle is the durable archive (the local `workbench/` file is disposable scratch).
2. **You write the scene.** There are no prebuilt vehicles, no styled scene components, no static image assets (fonts excepted). You compose the scene and author the GLSL shader yourself, against the core surface. The core gives you a canvas and the brand law; the picture is yours.

## The core surface (what you may import)

Everything comes from `src/remotion/cosmos.ts`. Nothing else exists to import; if you reach for a styled vehicle or a static image, it is gone on purpose.

- **`ShaderLayer`** + the **`GLSL`** snippet helpers — the GPU fragment-shader layer and the composable GLSL function strings (noise/`fbm`, `curlNoise`/`voronoi`/`domainWarp` for fluid/cellular/marbled fields, `dotField` for dense stipple, `sdf`/`sdf3d`/`raymarch` for 2D + 3D bodies, plus the presence set: `GLSL.sdfPresence` for subject anatomy — capsules, tapered cones, smooth carving, limited repetition — `GLSL.hiddenLineOcclusion` for line-field terrain, `GLSL.glowWithDirt` for light through a dirty medium, `GLSL.rampRetint` for the monotonic luma→hue retint). This is where you draw. `ShaderLayer` also takes an OPT-IN `bloom` prop for a real multi-pass emission glow — bloom the vehicle's OWN hot material, never a glued-on orb (doctrine 1) — and a `resolutionScale` prop that renders the canvas at a fraction of frame size for budgeting raymarched presence scenes (÷4 cost at 0.5; the upscale softness reads as the brand's degraded glass — cookbook §presence staging carries the budget discipline). See [references/cookbook.md](references/cookbook.md).
- **Audio hooks** — `useAudioReactivity`, `useEnergy`, `useBeat`, `useBass`, `useMid`, `useTreble`, `useOnset`, and `useAccumulated` (a pre-integrated leaky memory over an audio curve, pushed as a uniform — the deterministic way to give a world MEMORY: scorch that accumulates, ruins that stay lit after the drop) — the only legal source of audio reactivity (they read the `audio.*` curves). Three frequency bands (`useBass`/`u_bass` <150Hz kick, `useMid`/`u_mid` 150Hz-2kHz lead, `useTreble`/`u_treble` >2kHz hats) let you map different instruments to different elements — do that, rather than driving everything off one `energy` scalar. Prefer `useAudioReactivity` for the shared bus (`hit`, `swell`, `drop`, `disturbance`, the per-band signals, and ready shader uniforms) and then decide locally what material property — or, through a smoothed envelope, what motion — those signals disturb. NOTE: these only drive VISUALS — they make no sound.
- **`TrackAudio`** — plays the analysed clip so the render carries SOUND. **MANDATORY in every video:** drop `<TrackAudio audio={audio} />` in once. The hooks above only animate the picture; without this the cut is silent (the render asserts on silence and fails).
- **`useJourney`** — the narrative clock (`progress` / `phase` / `phaseProgress` / `arc`). One shared timeline for the whole scene.
- **Grain / texture is YOURS — no overlay is provided.** The core ships no `Grain` component and no `Starfield`. The recovered-footage degradation (the Light-Years Rule) is baked INTO your shader — use `GLSL.filmGrain` (plus dither/chroma-bleed) or roll your own, varied per video — and vary its AMOUNT and FORM to the composition. Grain is a signature, present and BOILING every frame, but its weight is the piece's call: heavy on a far-travelled relic, near-silent (bias the `intensity` LOW — but near-silent is a whisper, not silence; the floor is PERCEPTIBLE, doctrine 3) where a clean, electric finding wants its POP — an over-applied grain flattens the very pop it should protect. (The boil stays lively — each family its own rate; never crawl a grain's clock to game the gate — cookbook.) Likewise any background/field detail (a star drift, motes) you author yourself; nothing is imposed.
- **`TypePlate`** — the FIXED informational type system (identity lower-left, telemetry upper-right, prescriptive timing). Drop it once with scene-derived inks; never hand-roll the facts layout.
- **`FloatingType`** — the underlying typography primitive (the sanctioned roles + the ink-halo legibility guarantee). `TypePlate` and `CloseCard` consume it; reach for it directly only for `body` text a scene genuinely adds beyond the plate.
- **`CloseCard`** — the locked brand ending.
- **`paletteMix`** — derives the palette from the artwork swatches, SCENE-LED: `background` is a warm-dark drift (the one imposed rule — Warm Dark), `accent` is the artwork's OWN most-chromatic swatch (NO gold lean), `glow` is that accent's hot light, `ink` stays cream. `accent`/`glow` are light material for your shader; gold appears only if the artwork is genuinely warm — never imposed. (You may also skip it and build your palette from the raw `swatches` + the color helpers.)
- **color helpers** (`withAlpha`, `mix`, `luminance`, …), **fonts**, and the **props contract** types (`NostalgicCosmosProps` and friends).

That is the whole kit. The README has the exact signatures.

**Escape hatch (not part of the kit, not installed, not enforced):** if a concept genuinely needs a full 3D / React-Three-Fiber scene that even `sdf3d`+`raymarch` inside `ShaderLayer` cannot express, you can add `@remotion/three` + `@react-three/fiber` + `three` yourself — they are deliberately kept out of the kit. The raw-WebGL `ShaderLayer` is the default and almost always the right tool. If you reach for R3F, you own the install, determinism, and the grain / Light-Years degradation yourself.

## See what's already been made (your live reference)

You do not start blind, and nothing about prior work is hard-coded into this doc — it lives in the data, discoverable at runtime. The already-published videos are BOTH your diversity ledger AND your quality bar. Before you pick a vehicle:

- **Read the diversity ledger.** `bun run --cwd apps/cli fluncle admin tracks vehicles --json` lists the recently-used findings, newest first — each entry is `{ logId, addedAt, vehicle, grain, register, title, artists }`. That is the diversity ledger: vehicle, grain family, AND register (the third axis is now live; older entries have `register` absent). (Admin-gated, the same token the render upload uses; the public `/api/tracks` still carries `videoVehicle`, `videoGrain`, and `videoRegister` per finding for an unauthenticated read.)
- **Look at the actual posters — the immediate neighbour first.** Every published video exposes a poster image at `https://found.fluncle.com/<log-id>/poster.jpg`. FETCH the immediate found-date predecessor and the last ~3 and open them — `curl -s "https://found.fluncle.com/<log-id>/poster.jpg" -o /tmp/ref-<log-id>.jpg`, then view the file. Names tell you little; the pixels tell you everything — and the ledger entries are in feed order, so the top one is the thumbnail that will sit right next to yours.

Use them two ways: (1) pick a vehicle and a look clearly DIFFERENT from what you see — the immediate neighbour distinct in palette AND form AND contrast (doctrine 3), never the same medium twice in a row; (2) match or beat the best of them. As you look, the through-line is clear: the strongest Fluncle videos are flowing, organic, grainy, and alive (recovered footage), never clean, geometric, or CGI-polished — but a soft, blurry, low-contrast smear is where run after run collapses, so if the last one or two were that, hold the next one to legible structure with depth.

## Doctrine

Eleven rulings. They are LAW. If a concept fights one, change the concept. All of them serve the law above it: the whole frame is ONE integrated composition — fully abstract or recognizably-real-shown-abstractly — never a vehicle adrift on a dead background.

1. **One driver, one climax — inside the whole-frame composition; scene-led, NOT gold by default.** Within that one integrated frame, there is exactly ONE travelling, beat-synced driver — and that driver may be the **full-field surface ITSELF** (the screen is the driver, e.g. a vein-field or caustic wall that owns the canvas) OR a **focal subject that still fills/integrates the frame**, never a lone object floating on dead space. orb / lines / fractal / glass / glitch are EXAMPLES, not an enum — invented vehicles are welcome, provided there is exactly one. The video carries ONE committed CLIMAX moment — the "pop" — but it is **the vehicle's OWN material intensifying, in the SCENE's own palette, NOT a gold/sun effect bolted on.** Eclipse Gold is OPTIONAL here, never the default. A warm scene MAY let the vehicle's material run gold _only when the gold IS the vehicle_ (the vehicle's own crests/nodes going molten, never a glow on top); a cool or non-gold scene climaxes in ITS OWN colours — a deep-blue structure brightening, a chrome mass surging steel, a teal bloom resolving — and reaching for yellow there is the error. BANNED outright: the soft gold/yellow **"sun"** — a round bloom, halo, glow, or warm wash that appears regardless of the scene's palette. That bolted-on golden sun is the single most common way these clips go off-brand; cut it. Test the climax two ways: (a) delete the vehicle and the bright moment vanishes with it — there is no separate light source; (b) the climax colour is drawn FROM the scene's palette, never reached for because "Fluncle = gold." Its FORM is free (a shimmer, a crest, a resolving front, a surge, a single cell), never a default circular disc; its COLOUR is the scene's, gold included only when the vehicle's own material genuinely runs hot. One driver, one climax, free form and free colour, AND it is the driver itself. Two drivers means one is wrong. **The witness split (presence renders, 2026-07-04).** When the frame holds an indifferent subject (the three-clocks law, doctrine 7), the one DRIVER may be the reactive MEDIUM — the atmosphere, the light, the water, the treatment itself — while the SUBJECT is the still point the world breaks against. That is still one driver, not two: delete the subject and the climax loses its object; delete the medium and the reactivity dies. The climax is the world's reaction TO the subject — the light finding it, the fog parting around it, the shafts raking its face — never a second, competing reactive element; bind every atmospheric element to the subject spatially (fog pooling at ITS base, shafts breaking on ITS silhouette) so subject-and-weather read as ONE phenomenon.

2. **Always-present composition — presence from frame one, disclosure on the arc (amended 2026-07-04).** The composition fills the frame and is fully present from frame one to the last — never an empty frame waiting for the drop, never a composition that only begins mid-clip, and never a small mark adrift on dead space. A full-field surface is simply always there, edge to edge; a focal subject may fade/scale in over the first second, transform, travel, intensify — but even at its smallest it stays compositionally bound to the whole frame (its field, light, and grain are one world with the rest of the canvas). **Presence ≠ disclosure:** the subject is FELT from frame one — a silhouette in the fog, a mass in the atmosphere, a wake, an occluding darkness, unmistakably THERE — while full disclosure may come late and resolve across the arc. Occlusion is not absence: a darker-dark the fog wraps is present; a blank frame is not. The drop RESOLVES what was always there (the fog thins, the key light finds it, the dither snaps finer) — it never INTRODUCES; a reveal that starts from nothing is the dead intro wearing a costume. **And the scale axis is environment-vs-backdrop, not size:** a subject at any scale — including a small mass at great distance — is legal when the rest of the frame is an ENVIRONMENT (atmosphere with depth, parallax, a light relationship with the subject, living grain), and illegal when it is a BACKDROP (a flat gradient with nothing happening in it). Quiet is not dead — a vast dark sky that breathes with the bass is an environment. The sticker test: delete the subject; if the remaining frame is still a live world, it was never a sticker.

3. **Vehicle, grain, register AND structure diversity — judged on the PICTURE, anchored to the feed neighbour.** Findings post in found-date (Log ID) order, so the immediately-PRECEDING found-date finding is the video that sits RIGHT NEXT TO this one in the YouTube/TikTok grid. That neighbour is the HARD constraint, and it is never where the "music demands it" exception applies: this render MUST read clearly distinct from it in **palette AND form AND contrast**, not merely carry a different vehicle word. The ledger (`fluncle admin tracks vehicles --json`, newest-found first) lists those neighbours in feed order — but **judge the THUMBNAILS, not the labels:** FETCH AND VIEW the posters of the immediate predecessor and the last ~3, because two different vehicle words routinely render as the SAME soft smear and the eye reads palette/form/contrast, not your label. Distance-weight it: the immediate neighbour must be unmistakably different; a rhyme 4–5 findings back is acceptable. **Swing the structural register, don't repaint it.** Soft, blurry, low-contrast liquid — fog, smoke, membrane folds — is the basin these renders collapse into run after run; recolouring the same fog a new hue is NOT a new vehicle. If the previous one or two findings were that soft-liquid register, make THIS one legible hard structure WITH DEPTH and higher contrast (a dot-screen, dither/halftone, crisp-walled cellular, lines-with-depth). Sequential runs converge on the soft-liquid attractor the same way parallel runs converge on voronoi/horizontal-lines unless each deliberately diverges, so commit to a distinct medium and primitive. Honor the current brief and the failure modes below. Repeat a recent vehicle only if the music genuinely demands it — and never against the immediate neighbour. **Pick a grain FAMILY the same way** (cookbook §grain families): read `grain` across the recent ledger and choose one DISTINCT from recent runs, mapped to the texture/medium you chose (analog→VHS, dither→dither/halftone, fluent/watercolor→chemical-dye, nebula/orb→coarse-silver or fine-emulsion). Then set the grain AMOUNT to the composition — often below the 0.08 default, near-silent for a clean, electric, high-pop finding (an over-applied grain flattens the pop) — but near-silent is a WHISPER, not silence: the grain has a PERCEPTIBLE floor. "Bias low" has been over-learned to zero — run after run of shipped posters are vector-clean — so verify grain presence on the RENDERED poster/still viewed AT THUMBNAIL SCALE (the actual feed surface), never in the code; a frame with no visible grain anywhere fails the Light-Years Rule. Grain is always present and always boils, but its family and amount are yours — and name which family you used (and why) in the run report, the same discipline as the vehicle. **And rotate the REGISTER — the third diversity axis, presence-WEIGHTED (mix ruling, 2026-07-04).** Beside vehicle and grain, track which register each render lives in: **fully-abstract** (the field is the image), **representational** (nameable AND PLACED — a subject with a spatial relation to the observer, per the whole-frame law), or **framing-device** (a portal, the sleeve-as-logbook, a floating plate, a horizon — cookbook §framing devices). The operator has ratified subjects with presence as the direction, so the rotation is presence-weighted: when the recent window allows a swing, the representational register (staged per cookbook §presence staging) is the favoured reach — and pure-abstract fields STAY in rotation (a great field is still a great page; presence is the weighted default, never a monoculture). A register claim is judged on the PIXELS like everything else: the first representational claims shipped as relabeled texture fields (a voronoi net captioned "basalt columns"), which is why representational now means PLACED and must survive the stranger test on the shipped poster — a claim that reads as material texture only ships as `--register abstract`. If the last two findings were fully-abstract fields, reach for a placed subject or a framing device. Declare the register in the run report and pass it to ship as `--register <abstract|representational|framed>` — it lands in the ledger the same way vehicle and grain do. **And the STRUCTURAL family — the fourth axis, and the only one that is now MECHANICALLY CHECKED.** The vehicle NAME is free poetic identity ("crystal scaffold", "basalt organ", "tidal cell field") — but those three named three different worlds and were the SAME voronoi/cellular primitive underneath, so a name-only check let three cellular fields ship inside six. The fix reads the CODE, not the label: `ship` classifies the resolved shader body into a CLOSED, canonical set and records the dominant family in `render.json` (`structure`). That ledger column is the source of truth, and it is the shared vocabulary you author against — **cellular** (voronoi/worley cell fields: min-distance loops, F2−F1 walls, per-cell ids), **flow** (fbm / domain-warp / curl advection — marbled fields), **caustic** (sine-accumulator interference webs), **filament** (ridged-noise crests/veins: the `pow(1−abs(2n−1))` inversion), **lattice** (a regular grid / dot screen), **radial** (polar / kaleido fields), **metaball** (SDF blends / raymarched bodies), or **other**. `judge:diversity` gates on it: the SAME dominant family within the last 4 shipped findings is a hard FAIL; a repeat within the last 8 is a WARN. So invert the authoring order — **pick a family the recent window LACKS first, THEN name your world freely.** The family is the checked claim; the vehicle name stays the poetry on top of it. As of 2026-07-04 the window is SATURATED with **cellular** — it is the operator's least-favourite family and the basin the fleet keeps sliding back into (the crystal-scaffold / basalt-organ / tidal-cell-field trio, three cellular inside six), so treat cellular as spent for now and reach for flow / caustic / filament / radial / lattice / metaball instead. This is not an outright ban — a genuinely fresh cellular field is fair game once the window clears — but do not let it be your default reach. One nuance for presence renders: representational raymarched subjects CLUSTER structurally — a hull, a ruin, and a creature all classify as `metaball` (or `other`) even when the subjects are utterly different — so when BOTH entries in a same-family comparison are representational, the structural hit demotes from FAIL to WARN, and the poster distance (which sees the silhouette difference through edge orientation) plus the declared subjects carry the verdict. Subject diversity lives in the SILHOUETTE, which the fingerprint cannot see; rotate the subject kind (hull / ruin / flora / creature / terrain / threshold) the way you rotate everything else.

4. **Type is FIXED; the art is the variable.** Predictable information gets a predictable home: every clip renders its facts through **`TypePlate`** — identity (Artist — Title, with the release credit `Label (Year)` beneath) lower-left, telemetry (Found date + Log ID) upper-right, two categories that never share a stack (the release year is a catalog credit beside the label, never confused with Fluncle's own Found date) — and ends on the **`CloseCard`** at the same lower-left anchor. Timing is prescriptive (identity in ~1s, telemetry staggered after it, both clear before the drop so the climax plays on pure art); you may nudge the in-points onto a musical seam via the plate's props, never re-place the blocks or hand-roll the layout with raw `FloatingType`. What stays YOURS: **the inks** — drawn from the composition's own palette (its light, a swatch, a counter-accent), with emphasis welcome but always a colour FROM the scene, never a fixed brand colour; this includes the `CloseCard` signature (pass it `palette`), NOT a hard gold — the signature ink follows the scene like all other type. Every word passes VOICE.md ("Artist — Title", "Found Jun 4", sentence case, no exclamation marks); render only fields the props expose. Placement is law and the scene's character lives in the art and the ink, not in re-arranging the facts.

5. **Metadata must visibly matter.** Facts come from the track's Spotify metadata, surfaced in the props file — never from web search. (a) FACTS that may render on screen: release year and label (when present in the props). They are authoritative — straight from Spotify, the source of truth — so they need no citation and carry no alias-collision risk; render only fields the props actually expose, and never invent one. Rendering a verified fact is ENCOURAGED. (b) CREATIVE FUEL that never renders as text: the artwork palette the pipeline extracts, the album and label names, the track title, the shape of the energy curve, and — when the track is enriched — `track.features`, the enrichment spectral summary (`centroidHz` brightness, `subBassRatio` weight, `highRatio` air, `midFlatness` tonal-vs-noisy, `onsetRate` busyness). This is direction, not animation (the per-frame reactivity is the audio analysis) — it shapes your vehicle, texture family, palette lean, and scene concept: a sub-bass-heavy track wants a dense, heavy vehicle; a bright high-`centroidHz` track wants treble-driven sparkle and finer detail; a high `onsetRate` wants a busier, snappier field; a low `midFlatness` (tonal) leans melodic/fluent, a high one (noisy) leans gritty/dithered. It is OPTIONAL fuel — absent on un-enriched tracks, so degrade gracefully. (c) THE SECOND DIRECTION INPUT — `track.contextNote` + `track.texture` (the distilled context-note's character). When the finding has been context'd, the props carry the distilled `context_note` (firecrawl FACTS, dry prose) and, split out of its trailing `Texture:` line, `track.texture` — 3–6 sensory/scene/mood pointers (e.g. `["orchestrated", "layered", "expansive", "foundational", "intricate breakbeats", "atmospheric depth"]`). This is the missing SEMANTIC layer that sits ALONGSIDE the spectral features: **features = how it SOUNDS** (dense/bright/busy), **Texture = what it EVOKES** (orchestrated/atmospheric/foundational). Read both at concept time — the Texture shapes the same four levers (vehicle, texture family, palette lean, scene concept), now from meaning rather than spectrum: "atmospheric depth" + "expansive" leans a deep, layered volumetric field; "intricate breakbeats" leans a busier, finer structure; "nocturnal, rain-on-glass" leans a cool, wet, glassy palette and surface. Like features, it is OPTIONAL fuel — absent on un-context'd findings, so degrade gracefully (no Texture → steer off features + palette + names alone, exactly as before). It is direction ONLY: like the rest of (b), `contextNote`/`texture` are FUEL, **never on-screen text** — on-screen facts stay Spotify-sourced (a), so a Texture pointer never becomes a printed word, a citation, or a title overlay. **The divergence law (it informs, never templates):** the Texture is a lean, not a recipe — the same pointer must NOT collapse a parallel batch onto one look. "Layered/atmospheric" does not MEAN "another voronoi field"; let it nudge a genuinely distinct vehicle each run (the diversity ledger, doctrine 3, still rules). Your run report must name how the Texture drove a SPECIFIC visual decision — exactly as it already must for features — ("Texture: rolling, nocturnal, half-step menace → a cool half-lit caustic wall, no gold, dithered rain on the glass"), so the operator can see the meaning in the pixels and confirm it informed rather than templated. BPM is measured by the audio pipeline (beat-grid aligned, more accurate than any metadata tempo) — that is what the vehicle syncs to; musical Key, if the props expose it, is an optional DJ touch on the overlay, never required.

6. **The quad law (no printed rectangle, no porthole).** A `ShaderLayer` must never show its quad as a hard rectangle — handled two ways by layer type. A **full-bleed background** (the vehicle field) FILLS the frame: opaque, a real warm-dark value at every pixel INCLUDING the corners — it satisfies the law with nothing to fade. A **localized layer** that does not cover the frame (an orb, a glow, a bloom) fades color AND alpha to exactly 0.0 before its quad edge (a radial fade in layer space, r reaches 1.41 at the corners). Do NOT apply that radial/circular fade — or a tight `vignette` — to a full-bleed background: it turns the 1080×1920 canvas into a circular porthole. Gentle, off-centre, or directional edge-darkening for mood is fine; a centred radial crop is not.

7. **Motion law — GLOBAL translation is an audio-free constant clock; ALL reactivity is INTERNAL deformation.** A full-screen vehicle carries TWO motion channels, and they split. (1) GLOBAL TRANSLATION — the whole vehicle drifting past — is a CONTINUOUS, one-way, **audio-free constant-speed clock**: drive it from `u_time` / the journey `arc` / a steady eased rise alone, so the composition glides past at a fixed rate. (2) INTERNAL DEFORMATION — material morphing, lighting up, extending, inhaling and exhaling, flowing, luminosity rising on the drop, IN PLACE — is where **ALL** the audio reactivity belongs; this is "alive." Brightness, exposure, glow, width, thickness, threshold, warp amplitude, wall sharpness, curvature, density, grain may take the immediate, raw kick, because in-place deformation never translates and so can never reverse — bind the fast bands hard onto it (the gate-safe binding idiom, cookbook). The cleanest way to never reverse translation is to keep audio OFF it entirely; PREFER that. Audio may bend translation SPEED only with a SMALL coefficient over a DOMINANT positive constant base (the constant `u_time`/`sec`/`arc` term stays the bigger one), so velocity never goes negative — and a SMOOTHED signal only (the slow energy envelope, bar swells, the drop, or a beat run through an attack/decay), NEVER the raw per-beat transient and NEVER as the dominant term. So a kick makes a filament brighter and fatter at once (internal) while the whole field streams forward at a constant rate (global); it never jumps the field a step per kick. Keep procedural detail low-frequency enough (or fbm-softened) that it doesn't shimmer as it travels, and advance the density/noise phase MONOTONICALLY so the field roils forward in place without returning. The cure for motion that stutters is MORE smoothing — ultimately, audio entirely off translation (see the beat-jitter and audio-on-drift failure modes). And the symmetric cure: when the clip reads dead (the muted-audio test, doctrine 9), SHARPEN the internal bind — a fast band onto in-place structural deformation (the gate-safe binding idiom, cookbook) — never add motion. Smoothing fixes jitter; sharpening the material fixes deadness; the global clock is the lever for neither. **Exemplar:** `cold-murmuration` (round-2 018.5.7Y) — global motion is a pure constant clock, audio drives only the internal field. **This law is gated objectively:** after every render, `bun run --cwd packages/video judge:metrics <trackId>` measures the picture's short-lag motion reversal — whether it jumps then snaps back, jittering back and forth — and FAILS (exit non-zero) if it does. (`judge:metrics` folds the beat-pull score in beside the WCAG flash-safety hard gate — one report, both blockers; `detect-beat-pull` stays available as the fast directional read on the half-res draft while you iterate.) A crisp surge that hits ON the beat and flows on is fine; only TRANSLATION that UNDOES itself fails. The gate scores translation reversal and brightness-normalizes, so it is blind to material sharpness — sharp in-place deformation (width, threshold, glow, warp amplitude) never trips it. Stills cannot show translation reversal and a quick scrub can miss it, so the gate is the backstop that stops a law-breaking composition from shipping — a fail means a per-beat (or uncapped-swell) signal is driving position/travel; move that ONE bind into in-place material (width/threshold/glow/warp amplitude) and re-render. A pass is not a license to be calm: a clip that passes the gate but fails the muted-audio test is dead, not safe — sharpen the material bind (doctrine 9). **The three-clocks law (this law generalized to subjects — ratified 2026-07-04): the world answers the music; the Thing keeps its own clock.** In a presence render, the split runs one level up — the subject is to the scene what translation is to the field: the channel audio never touches — and the environment is the scene's material. Three clocks, three owners: the BEAT clock (kicks, hats) lands on environment MATERIAL only (`u_kickHit`, `u_snareHit`, `u_onsetPulse`, `u_air` — particulate, a wake, a rim, grain); the PHRASE clock (bars, the drop) owns the atmosphere's regime (`u_audioSwell`, `u_audioDrop`, `u_energy` — fog density, shaft ignition, transmittance); the SUBJECT keeps its own clock, audio-free (`u_time`, `u_progress`). The subject is STRICTLY INDIFFERENT (operator ruling, 2026-07-04): it never acknowledges the viewer or the music — never turns toward you, never flinches on a hat, never gains a pixel of speed at the drop. The world surging around a thing that does not care is what makes it read as a being rather than a visualizer puppet (at 174 BPM the hats sit near 8 Hz, an order of magnitude above any biological rate — a subject twitching there is comical, not alive). Two mechanical consequences, both measured: (1) **constant drift is MANDATORY on the subject — never lock it off.** Static content contributes nothing to the motion metric and masks nothing against the grain's reversal floor: a locked-off hero scored 0.20 (FAIL) and the same scene on a slow one-way drift/parallax scored 0.14 (PASS) with no change to the felt motion — and the drift IS the pass/approach the presence wants anyway. (2) **A breathing subject breathes on its own clock, in place:** implement the breath as internal SDF deformation (radius, curvature, silhouette width — an inhale, never a positional sway of the whole body), period ≥ 4s (the biological band), and let audio modulate AMPLITUDE only, through `u_audioSwell` with a small coefficient (≤ ~0.4 over the constant base) — never the rate or phase (a rate change is a velocity tick, the jitter signature). And every presence scene carries at least ONE fast environmental accent — `u_kickHit`/`u_snareHit` on motes, a wake, a rim — so the picture answers the beat while the subject holds its clock; an atmosphere-only scene reads dead to the beat-grid check on exactly the clips that feel most cinematic.

8. **Musical cut.** 20s default; choose `--duration-ms` (10–30s) from the waveform; end on a drop or just before a transition, never mid-build. The default is a FALLBACK, never a decision — every recent cut shipping at exactly 20.0s is the tell that nobody listened. The cut is a per-track musical call: read THIS track's waveform, choose, and name the choice (even "20s, because the drop resolves at 19.2s") in the report.

9. **The music leads, the video follows.** Audio reactivity is the point, not a finishing touch — the vehicle must visibly, legibly move WITH the track: lock to the beat grid, let the drop and the onsets HIT, map the energy curve to real swings in exposure / scale / width / density. The test, applied on the rendered MP4 (not the stills): **if the motion would look the same with the audio muted, the video has failed. The cure is to SHARPEN the material bind — route a fast band (`u_bassFast`/`u_midFast`/`u_trebleFast`, the transient-emphasized `u_kickHit`/`u_snareHit`, `u_audioHit`, `u_onsetPulse`) onto an in-place structural deformation (warp amplitude, ridge-survival threshold, cell/thread/filament width, voronoi wall sharpness) — never to add motion.** (The fine bands — `u_sub` <60Hz mass, `u_kickHit` the kick STRIKE, `u_snareHit` the snare CRACK, `u_air` >5kHz sparkle, plus `u_downbeatPulse` on the bar — let you place a DIFFERENT instrument on a different element than the broad `u_bass`/`u_mid`/`u_treble`; they are MATERIAL signals, never motion.) In-place deformation may be as sharp as the kick because it never translates, so it stays gate-safe; sharpening the bind is the symmetric counterpart to "the cure for jitter is more smoothing" (doctrine 7). Reach for the gate-safe binding idiom (cookbook) before touching travel. This stays inside the Motion law — reactivity rides MATERIAL (brightness, size, thickness, curvature) on the immediate beat AND may bend MOTION (travel, flow, convergence) too, but motion only through a SMOOTHED signal so the movement stays glassy and never snaps on the kick (doctrine 7) — and it must be FELT; ambient drift that merely coexists with the music is the failure this kills. The operating phrase: **audio disturbs the material and moves the picture — but motion only ever glides, never snaps.** At concept time, write a reactivity map with at least one structural reaction (width, density, radius, scale, thickness, curvature), one light reaction (glow, exposure, the climax's intensity), and one texture reaction (grain, dither, chroma, edge roughness) — and if any reaction drives MOTION, confirm it rides a smoothed envelope, never the raw transient. Sharp, structured textures carry this far better than soft haze. In a presence render (the three-clocks law, doctrine 7) all three reactions live on the ENVIRONMENT — fog density (structural), shafts/rim/ignition (light), grain/dither (texture) — the subject itself needs no binding at all, and at least one reaction is a FAST accent (`u_kickHit`/`u_snareHit` on particulate, a wake, a rim) so the beat lands in the world while the thing keeps its clock; presence changes where the reactivity GOES, never how much of it there is.

10. **The music dictates the shape.** The track's own arc is the clip's arc. Read the energy curve and the drop, and let the one vehicle evolve continuously with them — its density, structure, palette lean, exposure, and framing all rising, easing back, and cresting where the music does, building to the one climax (doctrine 1). Ease every change (`Easing.inOut(Easing.cubic)`) so it reads as the film breathing with the track. On a beat track this goes vivid as **the reactive scene-change (structural arc)** — the vehicle's whole CHARACTER transitioning across the song's sections, calm-before / vibrant-after (cookbook); the clip should feel like it MOVES THROUGH the song, not like one look twitching per beat.

11. **Compose to the vehicle's nature (symmetry + centre of gravity).** Two compositional disciplines, and they are CONDITIONAL — they apply only to vehicles that have a centre or an axis, never forced onto one that doesn't. (a) **Symmetry:** if the vehicle is symmetric — a kaleido/fractal fold, a radial mandala, a mirror-fold, a concentric or voronoi field grown from a centre — that symmetry HOLDS for the whole clip. It must not drift off-axis as it travels; a centre that wanders off to one side reads as broken, not designed. (b) **Centre of gravity:** if the vehicle has a single focal mass — a bloom, a body, a convergence, a dot-bloom — that mass sits either dead-centre OR at a decisive, obvious off-centre (rule-of-thirds, a corner); a mass that sits _slightly_ off-centre reads as a mistake, not a choice. **The exemption is real and important:** a translational / continuous-motion vehicle (a horizontal light-front, a sideways roll, a drifting field) has no single centre and no symmetry axis, so neither discipline applies; don't manufacture one. Decide which kind of vehicle you have, then commit to its composition.

## Failure modes (recognize them, they recur)

Not new law — the shapes of getting it wrong, so you can see them coming. These are the ways a render drifts off-brand that the rulings above don't spell out:

- **The polished-CGI / videogame look (the big one).** A vehicle that is too CLEAN, GEOMETRIC, and HARD-SHADED — a tidy Voronoi/stained-glass tiling, a flat raster grid, a smooth faux-3D solid — reads as "a 2010s 3D video game" or "an HTML canvas demo," NOT Fluncle. The brand is **lost footage recovered from outer space** (the Light-Years Rule): the texture must be lossy, grained, degraded, ALIVE. Two cures, used together: (1) **lean flowing-organic over sharp-geometric** — Fluncle's best work is flowing membranes, blobs, and sheets, never clean tilings or rasters; if a vehicle has a geometric skeleton, make it FLEX, curl, drift, and reform organically, never sit as a rigid lattice. (2) **Bake the Light-Years degradation INTO the composition** — heavy grain, dither, chroma-bleed, soft lossy edges, analog wobble — so it reads as recovered footage even at high render quality. The polish is NEVER the encoder's job: a clean shader at high quality still looks like CGI; a grain-soaked, flowing one looks FOUND. Test: if the frame could be a desktop wallpaper render or a game asset, it has failed — it must look like something Fluncle pulled out of a degraded transmission from the far edge of the map.
- **The clean-filter look (liquid chrome / Plastic-Wrap / stained-glass).** The polished-CGI failure's glossy sibling: a smooth, anti-aliased, GRAINLESS two-tone field — one accent colour on black, perfect gradients, specular sheen, vector-clean edges — that reads as a Photoshop filter pass (Plastic Wrap, Chrome, stained glass) over nothing. It escapes the videogame tells (no hard geometry, no flat shading) and still fails the same law: nothing about it looks FOUND. Same cures: bake the Light-Years degradation in (grain above the perceptible floor, dither, chroma-bleed, lossy edges) and give the gloss real structure and depth to sit on. Test: if the frame could be a filter-gallery preview swatch, it has failed.
- **The bolted-on golden sun.** A soft gold/yellow bloom, halo, glow, or warm wash that ignites no matter the scene's palette — reached for because "Fluncle = gold." BANNED (doctrine 1): the climax is the vehicle's own material intensifying in the SCENE's colours, and gold appears ONLY when the vehicle itself genuinely runs hot. A cool scene climaxes cool.
- **The fake glow (the gradient-orb / PowerPoint look).** A clean, smooth, lit-from-within glowing shape — a radial-gradient sphere, a softly-glowing ring, a tidy bloom — used as the bright focal element reads as a CSS `radial-gradient` or a PowerPoint glow: fake, weightless, non-organic. This bites even when it's NOT gold (a cool glow is just as fake) and even when the structure around it is good. The bright moment must be ORGANIC like the rest of the frame: carry grain, irregular or broken edges, internal filaments or dithered texture, a dissolve — never a clean gradient circle. Better still, make the bright moment the VEHICLE's OWN structure intensifying (the rings / blades / filaments themselves going hot and textured), not a separate smooth orb sitting at the centre. Test: if the bright element could be a CSS `radial-gradient()`, it has failed — degrade and texture it until it looks recovered, not rendered.
- **Lose the vehicle two ways — fog or chart.** A vehicle reads when it is STRUCTURE with DEPTH, and there are two opposite ways to lose it. A **formless fog**: soft warm gradients with no legible form — pretty, but nothing is actually _there_. A **flat chart**: thin, hard, sparse ruled primitives that reveal nothing — structure with no depth or meaning. The target is NOT a vague middle: commit to one pole of the whole-frame law — a fully abstract field WITH real depth, OR a recognizable thing rendered so the eye still names it — degraded/grained enough to read as found (see the videogame failure above, and the uncanny-middle failure below). The moodboard shows the register: a halftone dot-screen dissolving a bloom (`moodboard/halftone-tulip-bloom.png`), a CRT/scanline raster over a subject (`crt-scanline-roses.png`), contour lines that DISPLACE to sculpt a form out of an otherwise flat ruled field (`contour-eclipse-lines.png`) — sharp AND representational AND deep. Don't let "sharp" collapse to "horizontal lines + a horizontal gold bar"; vary the axis and primitive — dots, mesh, cellular, caustics, membranes — see the cookbook.
- **The uncanny middle (the half-real blob) — the big one.** The most common way a render dies now: it reaches for something real (a wing, a flower, a body) but lands on strange, unnatural shapes that resemble nothing — a blob with lines through it, neither a clean abstract field nor a recognizable thing. The middle lives at MID-DISTANCE in FULL LIGHT — detailed enough to demand anatomy, close enough to fail it. Decide your register up front and HOLD it: if representational, lean on the subject's ACTUAL structure (a real flock's streaming rivers and dark lanes, a real petal's curl, a real hull's mass) until the eye names it; the instant it starts reading as "weird abstract goo," retreat along the presence-preserving ladder (the whole-frame law): pull the subject to DISTANCE and SILHOUETTE first (a matte dark mass demands no anatomy), bury it deeper in the treatment second, and only then push ALL the way to a fully abstract field. "Almost real" is worse than "clearly abstract." If a stranger glancing at a frozen frame can't say either "that's abstract texture" or "that's a [thing]," you're in the middle — get out.
- **The floating vehicle (a subject on a BACKDROP — the axis is environment-vs-backdrop, not size).** A subject — a swarm, a bloom, a body — rendered as an isolated patch over an otherwise flat, static background (a plain gradient, an empty field). It reads as a sticker: disconnected, lonely, wrong. The failure is the DEAD SURROUND, not the subject's scale: a small dark hull at great distance is fully legal when the frame around it is a live ENVIRONMENT — atmosphere with depth and parallax, a light relationship with the subject, living grain (doctrine 2) — and a frame-filling blob is still a sticker when its surround is a flat gradient. Bind subject to world — fog pooling at its base, its grain, light, drift, and palette continuous with the entire canvas — so there is no "background," only one image. The sticker test: delete the subject; a live world remains, or it was never one image.
- **The presence killers (four anti-devices — each one undoes a staged subject).** (1) **The completed reveal:** the moment the whole body fits in frame it becomes an ASSET and shrinks to its literal pixels — keep the reveal partial, end the pass mid-crossing, let the fog re-swallow the glimpse; the pass never completes, and the off-frame continuation outlives the clip into the CloseCard. (2) **The dancing subject:** a subject that flinches on the hi-hat or gains speed at the drop is a puppet, not a presence — the music belongs to the witness and the world, never the thing (the three-clocks law, doctrine 7). (3) **The rendered lamp:** the fake-glow ban's presence form — draw an unexplained light source ONLY by its effects (forward scatter in the haze, rim agreement on every structure in frame, shadows snapping outward), never as the source itself; a gradient orb standing in for the cause kills the mystery that makes the effect work, and the light must read as the subject's own material (a slit in the hull igniting, a fire behind the ridge), never a separate lamp (doctrine 1). (4) **The centered colossus:** a subject centered and fully in frame is a specimen; presence needs the loaded edge — anchor the mass past the frame edge (partial occlusion by the frame is the strongest peek trigger: the body the eye completes is always bigger than one you could draw) or place it decisively off-centre with its contact points hidden in the fog.
- **The uniform wallpaper (the current attractor — the big one now).** A statistically stationary texture — the same density, brightness, and structure in every region, every crop interchangeable — is the sticker failure INVERTED: instead of a subject on dead space, a field with no subject anywhere. It satisfies every ban — warm dark ✓, one driver ("the field") ✓, no gold ✓, no sticker ✓ — and moves nobody: it reads as a Photoshop filter or a tileable texture swatch, not a phenomenon observed. This is where the fleet actually collapsed: run after run of "glowing net on black," recoloured — the labels diverse, the pixels not. The cure is the depth + landing-point law (the whole-frame law above): give the field a real depth mechanism, privilege ONE region — denser, brighter, deeper — and put the climax where the eye lands. **The corner-swap test:** if the frame's four corners could be swapped without anyone noticing, it is wallpaper.
- **The copy-paste climax.** Every clip blooming bright in the dead centre and settling — the climax landing as a centred mid-clip glow every time. The moment is always present and always ONE, but its TIMING, PLACEMENT, FORM, and COLOUR are yours: ignite late, build slow and hold, sweep across as a front, sit off-centre, pulse and recede, crest at the very end. `useJourney`'s depart→travel→arrive with a centred settle is ONE option, not a mandate. If your bright moment lands where the recent posters' did, move it.
- **The scripted-clock arc.** A JS `interpolate(sec, …)` timeline pinned to a hardcoded `peakTimeMs` driving the STRUCTURE directly (the density, threshold, width, or ignition stepping off a `sec` index instead of the live signal). It aligns with the music only on the one track it was tuned to — on the next track the drop lands elsewhere and the structure crests in the wrong place. Drive the structural arc from the live envelopes (`u_energy`/`u_audioSwell`/`u_audioDrop`) instead; the only sanctioned home for a drop timestamp is the `reactivity.drop` envelope config, which derives a smoothed `u_audioDrop`. And you rarely need to supply one: the analyzer now ships `audio.dropMs` (a breakdown→slam novelty detection) and the drop envelope DEFAULTS its peak to it — an explicit `reactivity.drop.peakTimeMs` still wins, but you no longer hand-derive the drop from energy-curve peaks unless you're overriding. Keep `interpolate(sec, …)` for the journey baseline (drift, a slow open, type seams), not the structure. This law now has a MECHANICAL form at ship time: the scene emitter (Unit S, `scene.json`) statically scans the shader body, and any custom `uniform` beyond the header set — the tell of a clip-time JS driver like `u_rise`/`u_settle` — marks the scene **not live-ready** (`liveReady: false`), because a live host has no way to drive it. A body that reads **header uniforms only** (drive the arc off `u_progress` / the live envelopes) is what replays exactly behind the decks. See the live-ready authoring rule in `references/workflow.md` §4.
- **The porthole.** A tight radial `vignette` (or a circular `edgeFade`) on the full-bleed background darkens the corners until the 9:16 frame reads as a circle — the quad-law fade meant for a localized orb, misapplied to the whole field. The background must reach the frame's edges and corners; keep any vignette gentle and off-centre, never a centred radial crop (doctrine 6).
- **Copy-paste ink, and clashing ink.** Placement is fixed (doctrine 4: the `TypePlate` owns it) — what must NOT be copy-pasted is the COLOUR. Derive the ink from the scene's own palette — a dusty rose, an artwork counter-accent, the scene's own light. **Match the ink to the palette where the type ACTUALLY SITS — especially the `CloseCard`: pull from the scene's ENDING/dominant colours, not a transient early accent** (a cool ink chosen from a cold opening reads wrong over a warm close, and vice-versa). `FloatingType` carries its own contrast guarantee, so a non-cream ink still reads. Cream is a safe default; the type is never the brand gold — its ink follows the composition, the `CloseCard` included.
- **Beat-jitter — over-correcting the reactivity (flow, don't strobe).** Reactivity must be FELT (doctrine 9), but the common over-correction is pulsing the material HARD on every quarter-beat, so it strobes and jitters instead of flowing — at worst an actual epilepsy risk. The cure is not LESS reactivity but SMOOTHER, less literal reactivity: (1) make the PRIMARY pulse the slow energy ENVELOPE and bar-level swells, not the per-beat transient; (2) keep per-beat / onset hits SUBTLE in amplitude and SMOOTHED with an attack/decay so each lands as a soft swell, never an instant snap; (3) don't default every reaction to the 1/4-beat grid — vary the rhythmic target (the bar, the phrase, the energy shift) so the whole thing breathes rather than ticks. The clip must FLOW; if the material lurches or stutters between beats, you've over-corrected. This is the same principle doctrine 7 puts on MOTION (audio may move things only through a smoothed signal, never the raw beat) applied to MATERIAL: even legal material reactivity must be smoothed, not strobed. **And cap the CLIMAX exposure:** never let the peak wash the whole frame to white — clamp so even the hottest material stays below the cream stop and keeps its texture; a frame blown to flat white is the strobe at its worst (it killed a recent climax).
- **Audio on the global drift (the DJ-scratch).** Binding an audio uniform to the whole vehicle's translation with NO constant base — `float drift = u_audioSwell * 0.10;` — makes the drift VELOCITY follow `d(swell)/dt`, which goes negative whenever the swell dips, so the entire composition slides forward then jumps backward: a literal DJ scratch (the comment may claim "one-way drift," but the code is not one-way). The cure is doctrine 7: drift is a DOMINANT constant-speed clock (`u_time`/`sec`/`arc`), audio off translation entirely, all reactivity moved INTERNAL.
- **The uncapped-swell drift surge.** A large `swell * k` on any drift / travel / position / speed term — `drift = sec*0.85 + swell*1.4 + drop*0.5`, where the audio coefficient rivals or exceeds the constant `sec`/`u_time` term — surges the WHOLE vehicle on the beat. The swell is uncapped (it reaches 1.0 across a beat track), so its derivative is large and the global motion lurches or reverses on every hit. The cure is the same: keep the constant-clock base DOMINANT, prefer audio off translation, and move the reactivity into in-place internal deformation (the gate-safe binding idiom, cookbook).
- **The mild render — rule-compliant but timid (the whole-clip failure).** Every failure mode above is a way to be WRONG; this is the way to be FORGETTABLE. A clip can satisfy every rule — warm dark ✓, one vehicle ✓, grain ✓, no blowout/strobe/gold ✓ — and still be dull: a few sparse elements drifting on near-dead space, nothing alive, nothing that grabs. **Rule-compliant ≠ good.** The fences against blowout / strobe / fake-glow / gold / CGI, OVER-applied, actively PRODUCE timidity — you back away from every bold move until nothing is left. Bias toward the bolder, more-alive idea: a dense field that owns the frame, a vehicle with real internal motion and depth, reactivity you can FEEL (the whole-composition twin of "reactivity must be FELT", doctrine 9). The test is the doom-scroll: would a thumb stop, or keep going? If it reads as safe, push it — the taste pass (workflow step 8) applies exactly this at ship time, the new poster NEXT TO its three feed neighbours at thumbnail size. **When it reads safe or fails the muted-audio test, sharpen the MATERIAL bind: route the fast bands onto in-place structural deformation (warp amplitude, ridge threshold, cell/thread/filament width, wall sharpness) — the gate-safe binding idiom (cookbook) — so the structure visibly deforms on the kick.** "Alive" is bought with MATERIAL (brightness, width, scale, glow, in-place deformation on the beat) and SMOOTHED motion, never with raw beat-locked MOTION. Sharpen the material bind, not the motion; the field roils and the cores flare on the drop, the picture surges on the bass swell — and it never snaps back on the kick.

## The constants you cannot touch

Grain / recovered-footage degradation on every frame, baked into your shader (the core ships no Grain overlay — it's yours, varied per video; its AMOUNT and FORM are the composition's call — bias the `intensity` low for an electric finding, never a fixed heavy wash, and NEVER to zero: the grain must stay perceptible on the rendered frame at thumbnail scale, or the Light-Years Rule is broken — and the BOIL stays lively, each family its own rate, never crawled to game the gate). One committed CLIMAX moment, expressed through the vehicle's own material in the SCENE's palette — gold OPTIONAL, never the default, never a bolted-on "sun" (doctrine 1). Oxanium for brand marks and numerals only. `Artist — Title` with the one sanctioned em dash. `Found Jun 4` dates (UTC, tabular, never released/added). Warm darks only. The `CloseCard` ending. Determinism: remotion seeded `random()` and frame-derived values only — never `Math.random` / `Date.now` / `new Date()`. The Retint Rule: gold is one accent among the scene's palette, never forced as a "sun"; the type is never the brand gold — its ink follows the composition (the scene's ending palette), the `CloseCard` included.

## Commands

Run from `packages/video` (or `bun run --cwd packages/video <script>`). Use `bun`, never npm/pnpm/yarn. Exact flags live in `packages/video/README.md`.

- `bun run social:preview <trackId> --skip-render` — resolve + analyze audio (BPM, beat grid, onsets, energy + bass/mid/treble band curves), extract artwork palette, write `out/<trackId>.props.json`. Run first; the props file is your ground truth.
- `bun run social:preview <trackId> --composition <CompId>` — the full pipeline through render to `out/<trackId>.mp4`, pointed at your composition.
- `bunx remotion still src/remotion/index.ts <CompId> out/still-N.png --props=out/<trackId>.props.json --frame=N` — the stills for the critique loop (GPU shaders need a GL context: ANGLE locally / swangle on a GPU-less host, inherited from `remotion.config.ts` via `FLUNCLE_GL` — no `--gl` flag needed).
- `bun run studio` — Remotion Studio, to scrub a scene live with default props.
- `bun run --cwd packages/video typecheck`, `bunx oxlint packages/video`, and `bun run --cwd packages/video lint:composition src/remotion/workbench/<CompId>.tsx` — the three pre-render gates. The composition lint is the static motion check: it flags any global translation term binding audio over (or without) a dominant constant clock (doctrine 7's whole-vehicle JUMP bugs) and exits non-zero on a finding. Format with `bunx oxfmt --write <files>`, never prettier.
- `bun run --cwd packages/video detect-beat-pull out/<trackId>.draft.mp4` — the FAST directional beat-pull read for the draft loop only; never the ship verdict.
- `bun run --cwd packages/video judge:metrics <trackId>` — THE mandatory post-render gate, run on every full render before ship. One report (`out/<trackId>.metrics.json`) carrying THREE hard gates — beat-pull (Motion law, doctrine 7), the WCAG 2.3.1 flash-safety photosensitivity gate, and arc/deadness (the structural twin of beat-pull on the LONG timescale: it fails a frozen field / looping wallpaper that never reorganizes across the clip) — plus the advisory coupling and intent reads. Exits non-zero on any hard fail; a fail BLOCKS ship, no exceptions, never `--allow-flash` on a ship candidate.
- `bun run --cwd packages/video validate:intent out/<trackId>.intent.json` — the STRICT lint on your render-intent file (step 7): precise per-field errors on the schema, the enum fields (`textureFamily`/`register`/`arcSource`/`motionModel`), and every `bindings[]` entry. Run it after you write the intent, before the render; exit `0` valid, `1` invalid, `2` usage error.
- `bun run --cwd packages/video judge:diversity <log-id>` — two axes at once (doctrine 3). (1) The POSTER's structural distance from its recent published feed neighbours (edge-orientation-dominant, so a recolour of the same form scores CLOSE) — advisory, a low distance to the immediate neighbour is the objective read on "swing the structural register, don't repaint it." (2) The SHADER STRUCTURAL family (the checked claim the vehicle name can't carry): it classifies the subject + the recent ledger and FAILS when your dominant family repeats inside the last 4 shipped findings (WARNs inside the last 8), naming the repeat — `basalt organ (cellular)`; a same-family hit where both entries are representational demotes to WARN (raymarched subjects cluster in `metaball`/`other` — doctrine 3). Historical entries without a recorded `structure` are classified on the fly from their fetched `composition.tsx`, or skipped when unreachable. Pass `--assume-structure <family>` to dry-run the gate against a hypothetical family, `--strict` to make a repeat a non-zero exit.
