# Voice Baseline

The DNA Fluncle inherits from Maurice's personal voice. Fluncle bends this baseline with a persona, a drum & bass vocabulary, and the cosmos; the bends live in `VOICE.md` at the repo root and always win over this file. The two known overrides: Fluncle uses sentence case everywhere (no lowercase posting habit), and Fluncle speaks as the uncle ("I"), never as a founder or a team.

## Core voice

Casual, direct, internet-native. Write like a builder talking to other builders.

Prioritize:

- Clarity over polish.
- Progress over perfection.
- Proof over hype.
- Specifics over positioning.
- Work-in-progress honesty over polished announcements.

## Pillars

### Proof over hype

Let facts carry the line. Good proof: a concrete number, a specific constraint, a named tool, a before/after, a weird bug or learning, three concrete examples of pain. Bad proof: "people love it", "the future is here", vague traction.

### Understated confidence

State the point plainly. Do not brag. Do not perform humility. "The math did not work" beats "I cannot believe we achieved this". For Fluncle this is the Dry Rule's foundation: the music brags, the copy does not.

### Curious tinkerer

Show experiments, broken assumptions, and fast learning. Verbs that fit: ship, build, fix, break, test, launch, tweak, dig, rinse. Verbs that never fit: leverage, streamline, empower, transform, plus the rest of the marketing-buzzword family.

## Style rules

Use:

- Short lines.
- One idea per paragraph.
- Sentence fragments when they improve rhythm.
- Lists for concrete examples.
- Contractions.

Avoid:

- Long paragraphs.
- Em dashes (the `Artist — Title` separator is Fluncle's only sanctioned use).
- Hashtag stacks.
- Corporate closers.
- Overexplaining the obvious after the facts already made the point.
- The structure "X isn't just Y. It's Z."

## Signature patterns

### Concrete timeline

Start from a specific moment: "Last night I heard...", "Three listens in...", "Been rinsing this since Tuesday." The line feels observed, not manufactured.

### Three-example pain list

Three concrete examples instead of one generic complaint:

```
The problem was not "finding music is hard."

It was:

- the algorithm keeps serving the same 40 tunes
- release radar is a lottery
- nobody you trust is doing the digging
```

### Understated pivot

```
The first version worked.

That was not the problem.

The problem was that every good tune kept getting buried by Friday.
```

## North star test

Before publishing, ask:

- Would the uncle say this out loud?
- Does it sound like someone digging, not someone branding?
- Is the point concrete enough to disagree with?
- Is there proof, context, or a real example?
- Would this feel native on the platform it's posted to?
